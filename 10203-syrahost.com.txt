<div class="title-h3 _align-left">Learn about Domain Names</div>
</div>
<div class="g-accordion-version-3 accordion">
<div class="accordion-element section expanded">
<div class="accordion-title heading">

<span class="text">Australian domain name options</span>
</div>
<div class="accordion-content article" style="display: block;">

<p>Every country has its own domain extension, which is an important part of your full web address. The Australian country extension is <span class="bold">.au</span>, and <span class="bold">.com.au</span> is the most popular option for businesses.</p>
<p>There are also extensions for two Australian cities – <span class="bold">.sydney</span> and <span class="bold">.melbourne</span> – that have become very popular for local targeted businesses.</p>
<p>Here are the Australian domain names you can choose:</p>
<ul class="g-list _bullpoint">
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.com.au</span> – This is the classic option for businesses. It is also the default that many people type in their browsers, so it’s a good idea to reserve it for your business’s address.</li>
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.net.au</span> – A common choice for business websites or technology firms. It’s also a good option if the .com.au version is taken.</li>
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.org.au</span> – Meant for non-profit charities and organisations.</li>
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.asn.au</span> – Open for political parties, trade unions, sporting clubs, charities, incorporated associations and non-profits.</li>
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.id.au</span> – Meant for personal websites for Australian citizens and residents. They cannot be used for businesses.</li>
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.sydney</span> – For businesses and people located in Sydney, NSW.</li>
<li class="list-item"><span class="bold">.melbourne</span> – For businesses and people located in Melbourne, VIC.</li>
</ul>
<p>Having an Australian web address gives you a local presence for your business and brand. It also makes it easy for users to find and identify you in search engines as having a recognisable Australian address.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="accordion-element section web-hosting-info">
<div class="accordion-title heading">

<span class="text">How to choose the right domain name</span>
</div>
<div class="accordion-content article">

<p>Choosing a domain name is a key step to having a successful online presence. Domain names are also known as web addresses, and they represent your online business, project or blog just like your street address represents your physical location. The following tips will help you choose the right one.</p>
<p class="bold">Refer to your business</p>
<p>If your business is called Kev of all Trades, then the name kevofalltrades.com.au is a natural choice. If it’s not available, then you can try other variations like alltrades.com.au, koat.com.au or even kevofalltrades.net.au. If you’re located in Sydney or Melbourne you could even look at kevofalltrades.sydney or the .melbourne variation. Just make sure you use some reference of your name, brand or idea.</p>
<p class="bold">Keep it short and sweet</p>
<p>The shorter your domain, the easier it is to remember. Abbreviations or half abbreviations like koat.com.au are very popular.</p>
<p class="bold">Be unique</p>
<p>Choose a name that represents you or your products. Don’t register an address similar to a competing business or website with the same products or content.</p>
<p class="bold">Make it local</p>
<p>Choose a geographical extension that represents where your business is located. For example, .com.au is used for websites in Australia and .co.uk is used for websites in Britain. Each country has its own popular type.</p>
<p class="bold">Protect your brand</p>
<p>Register multiple domains and direct them all to your main site to protect your brand. If you have registered .com.au, registering .com as well will stop anyone from using your brand. Make sure your address looks professional and don’t use a bunch of numbers, hyphens and slang words. Remember, this will be part of your email addresses, so you want to look good.</p>
<p class="bold">Can’t decide among your options?</p>
<p>There are no limits to how many domains you can register. Many business will register multiple versions and have them all redirect to one website or park them for future projects.</p>
</div>
