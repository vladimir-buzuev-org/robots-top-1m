			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2012/03/osmf-2-0-now-available.html" title="Permalink to OSMF 2.0 now available!" rel="bookmark">OSMF 2.0 now available!</a></h2>
			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2012/03/osmf-2-0-now-available.html" title="3:59 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date  updated">March 23, 2012</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/author/dothomas" title="View all posts by dothomas" rel="author">dothomas</a></span> | <span><a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2012/03/osmf-2-0-now-available.html#comments">Comments</a> (6)</span>
			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
			<div class="entry-content">
				<div class="at-above-post-homepage addthis_tool" data-url="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2012/03/osmf-2-0-now-available.html"></div><p>The online video world continues to grow at breakneck speed and we, at Adobe, have been fortunate to be a big part of making it happen. More video content is available today than ever before and the trend is only accelerating. The World Cup drew a record number of online viewers online and engagement levels were the highest. Users could switch multiple camera angles, watch highlights and watch on demand. I am looking forward to seeing more content, more engaging experiences and higher quality.</p>
<p>Adobe&#8217;s mission has been to enable you to create great content and bring it to your audiences. We are thrilled to announce the launch of OSMF 2.0. The new version of OSMF 2.0 allows you to create even more engaging experiences for your audience. We worked with some of the largest content owners with the largest online video audiences and have reworked the inner workings of OSMF. In particular, we have made major changes to the Adaptive Bit Rate (ABR) algorithm and the APIs for ABR.</p>
<p>The OSMF Adaptive Streaming provides two modes: i) Manual switching and ii) Automatic switching. As part of the changes, we have improved the automatic switch operation; added more information to the Quality of Service (QoS) information and history; enhanced the metric base, factory and repository; and finally optimized the rule engine to better handle bandwidth and other computer resources. More details on the changes can be found here:</p>
<p><a title="OSMF developer site" href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Main_Page" target="_blank">http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Main_Page</a></p>
<p>We hope you are as excited about this release as we are. As always, we look forward to hearing your feedback on this latest release. Thanks for your support.</p>
<p>Kelash Kumar, Group Product Manager, Video Solutions</p>

			<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/09/announcing-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback-1-6.html" title="Permalink to Announcing OSMF and Strobe Media Playback 1.6" rel="bookmark">Announcing OSMF and Strobe Media Playback 1.6</a></h2>
			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/09/announcing-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback-1-6.html" title="7:09 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date  updated">September 8, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/author/cathi-kwon" title="View all posts by Cathi Kwon" rel="author">Cathi Kwon</a></span> | <span><a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/09/announcing-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback-1-6.html#comments">Comments</a> (9)</span>
			</div><!-- .entry-meta -->
			
			<div class="entry-content">
				<div class="at-above-post-homepage addthis_tool" data-url="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/09/announcing-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback-1-6.html"></div><p>2011 has already been an exciting year in the world of online video. <a href="http://youtube-trends.blogspot.com/2011/08/old-spice-guy-vs-fabio-draws-millions.html" target="_blank">Fabio&#8217;s battle with the Old Spice Guy</a>, the realization of <a href="http://www.adobe.com/aboutadobe/pressroom/pressreleases/201109/090811AdobePassTVEverywhere.html" target="_blank">TV Everywhere</a>, user-generated videos and the Arab Spring (not to equate the importance of any of these!) &#8211; these all testify to online video&#8217;s ability to resurrect careers, entertain, and literally change the world.</p>
<p>As online video evolves, the technology that supports publishing video must follow. With that objective in mind, I am very proud to announce the release of OSMF and Strobe Media Playback 1.6. Over the past several months, you have provided us with valuable feedback, and that became the foundation for this release.</p>
<p><strong>What&#8217;s new?</strong></p>
<p><strong>Mobile</strong>: Video usage on smartphones and tablets continues to grow rapidly. Just today, Knowledge Networks published that video viewing on mobile devices among 13 to 54 year olds has <a href="http://www.knowledgenetworks.com/news/releases/2011/090811_ott-video.html" target="_blank">doubled over the last year</a>. As part of the 1.6 release, we have included new features that will help you bring your content to mobile devices.</p>
<ul>
<li>OSMF: <a title="stage video support" href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Stage_Video" target="_blank">Stage Video</a> support for hardware-accelerated video presentation (requires Flash Player 10.2+)</li>
<li>Strobe Media Playback: <a title="Smartphone and tablet chrome" href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=HTML5_%26_JavaScript_support_for_SMP" target="_blank">Smartphone and tablet optimized chrome</a> for Flash-enabled devices</li>
<li>Strobe Media Playback: <a title="HTML5 video player" href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=HTML5_%26_JavaScript_support_for_SMP" target="_blank">HTML5 video player</a> for non-Flash devices</li>
<li>Strobe Media Playback: <a title="JavaScript device detection" href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=HTML5_%26_JavaScript_support_for_SMP#Including_the_necessary_JavaScript_.26_CSS_library_files" target="_blank">JavaScript device detection</a> library for selecting the appropriate player and chrome</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Enhanced Viewer Experience</strong>: One of OSMF&#8217;s core objectives has always been to enable you to create engaging experiences for your viewers. Towards that end, we have released the following features.</p>
<ul type="disc">
<li>OSMF: <a href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Live_DVR_Rolling_Window_for_HTTP_dynamic_streams" target="_blank">DVR rolling window</a> support, which allows you specify how far back from the live point viewers can rewind (requires <a href="http://www.streamingmedia.com/Articles/News/Featured-News/Flash-Media-Server-4.5-Delivers-to-iPad-%26-iPhone-77490.aspx" target="_blank">the newly released FMS 4.5</a>)</li>
<li>OSMF: <a href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Late-Binding_Audio" target="_blank">Late-binding audio</a> for providing viewers with multiple audio track options, e.g. additional languages, director&#8217;s commentary, and &#8220;home&#8221; v. &#8220;away&#8221; coverage</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Core Framework</strong>: Beyond new functionality, we continue to improve the existing core framework. In OSMF 1.6, we have made improvements to HTTP Dynamic Streaming as well as the ability to better manage bitrate profiles with <a href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Flash_Media_Manifest_(F4M)_File_Format#The_multi-level_manifest" target="_blank">multi-level manifests</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Documentation</strong>: We have made the first major step in improving our documentation with the launch of a <a href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Main_Page" target="_blank">completely revamped wiki</a> for OSMF and Strobe Media Playback. We hope this will make finding information and resources on specific topics easier, and I encourage you to bookmark it for future reference.</p>
<p><a href="http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe/index.php?title=Main_Page" target="_blank">http://sourceforge.net/apps/mediawiki/osmf.adobe</a></p>
<p>We look forward to hearing your feedback on this latest release. As always, you can reach me at ckwon at adobe dot com. The <a href="http://forums.adobe.com/community/opensource/osmf/" target="_blank">developer forums</a> are also great venues for discussion, and the team regularly monitors these as well.</p>
<p>Thanks for your continued support!</p>
<p>Cathi, OSMF Product Manager</p>

<p>Late-binding audio is currently available for VOD only.  We are also working on live/linear support, which will be available in our next drop.</p>

 pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;MediaPlayer&quot;, mediaPlayer);
 pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;MediaContainer&quot;, mediaContainer);

 // Configure the plugin with the ad information
 // The following configuration instructs the plugin to play a mid-roll ad after 20 seconds
 pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;midroll&quot;, &quot;http://gcdn.2mdn.net/MotifFiles/html/1379578/PID_938961_1237818260000_women.flv&quot;);
 pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;midrollTime&quot;, 10);

 // Uncomment the following lines to see a pre-roll, overlay, and post-roll ad.
 // pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;preroll&quot;, &quot;http://gcdn.2mdn.net/MotifFiles/html/1379578/PID_938961_1237818260000_women.flv&quot;);
 // pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;postroll&quot;, &quot;http://gcdn.2mdn.net/MotifFiles/html/1379578/PID_938961_1237818260000_women.flv&quot;);
 // pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;overlay&quot;, &quot;http://gcdn.2mdn.net/MotifFiles/html/1379578/PID_938961_1237818260000_women.flv&quot;);
 // pluginResource.addMetadataValue(&quot;overlayTime&quot;, 20);

 // Once the plugin is loaded, play the media.
 // The event handler is not needed if you use the statically linked plugin,
 // but is here in case you load the plugin dynamically.
 // For readability, we don’t provide error handling here, but you should.
 mediaFactory.addEventListener(
    MediaFactoryEvent.PLUGIN_LOAD,
    function(event:MediaFactoryEvent):void
    {
       // Now let's play the video - mediaPlayer has autoPlay set to true by default,
       // so the playback starts as soon as the media is ready to be played.
       mediaPlayer.media = mediaElement;
    });

// Load the plugin.
mediaFactory.loadPlugin(pluginResource);</p></div>

<h2>Implementation details</h2>
<p>We chose to use the MediaPlayer API instead of the ProxyElement, SerialElement, or ParallelElement APIs. There are a few reasons why we wrote custom control code instead of using the composition APIs:</p>
<ul>
<li>We have full control over the pre-buffering of the ads.</li>
<li>We don&#8217;t change the properties or traits of the main media.</li>
<li>We wanted to provide an example of how the MediaPlayer API can be used inside an OSMF plug-in.</li>
</ul>
<p>The plug-in relies on getting references to both the MediaPlayer and the MediaContainer instances. The player is responsible for passing them to the plug-in as configuration metadata.</p>
<p>Implementing a mid-roll scenario involves the following steps:</p>
<ol>
<li>Pre-buffer the ad.</li>
<li>Once the ad is ready to be played, pause the main video.</li>
<li>Remove the main video from the MediaContainer and add the ad to it instead.</li>
<li>Once ad playback has finished, remove the ad from the MediaContainer and add the main video back.</li>
<li>Resume main video playback.</li>
</ol>
<p>For displaying an overlay ad, we simply add the ad to the same MediaContainer and configure its layout.</p>
<p>These are some starting points. We encourage you to check the source code, and let us know what you think.</p>

			<div class="entry-meta">
				<span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">Posted on</span> <a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/02/getting-started-with-stage-video-support-in-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback.html" title="9:09 pm" rel="bookmark"><span class="entry-date  updated">February 11, 2011</span></a> <span class="meta-sep">by</span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/author/andrian-cucu" title="View all posts by Andrian Cucu" rel="author">Andrian Cucu</a></span> | <span><a href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/02/getting-started-with-stage-video-support-in-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback.html#comments">Comments</a> (10)</span>

			<div class="entry-content">
				<div class="at-above-post-homepage addthis_tool" data-url="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/02/getting-started-with-stage-video-support-in-osmf-and-strobe-media-playback.html"></div><div>
<h2 id="internal-source-marker_0.22100810962729156">Introduction</h2>
<p>The guiding principle of the Stage Video implementation in OSMF is that the current API should allow a video player developer to upgrade to the latest version of OSMF and get the performance boost of Stage Video without having to modify their existing OSMF-based video players.  For more on the what, why, and how of Stage Video in OSMF, please check out our other blog post, <a title="Stage Video in OSMF" href="http://blogs.adobe.com/osmf/2011/02/stage-video-in-osmf.html">Stage Video in OSMF</a>.</p>
<p>Here I’d like to describe the new Stage Video-related features in Strobe Media Playback which you can use for troubleshooting your setup. After this, we’ll guide you through the process you can use for testing your video player with the latest OSMF codebase.</p>
<h2>Strobe Media Playback &#8211; Watching Stage Video in Action</h2>
<p>We have published a live Stage Video demo in our dev space on osmf.org. See it in <a href="http://osmf.org/dev/latest/debug.html?src=rtmp://cp67126.edgefcs.net/ondemand/mp4:mediapm/osmf/content/test/sample1_700kbps.f4v&amp;logFilter=StageVideo">here</a>.</p>
<p>The player is instructed to show the Info Overlay at startup, so you should see the current version of the Flash Player installed in your browser. You can also see if the hardware-accelerated decoding and rendering is being used or not.</p>
<p>If you don’t have a version of Flash Player with Stage Video capability installed, the player will let you know about it:</p>
<p><img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/SwC4JLdzBDPcsbdeU61NpBRapn8tMZ7niBTFmtz73k55KamI8RuwBtIMeFJte-5Buw1vyJjeXpdhS57FgZ9IUxYp-_BJETFLoADylqxOsaPrdPf9DFxj9G7X9_nBJBY" alt="" width="639px;" height="479px;" /></p>
<p>You can download a version of Flash Player with Stage Video support here:<br />
<a href="http://get.adobe.com/flashplayer/">http://get.adobe.com/flashplayer/</a></p>
<p><a href="http://get.adobe.com/flashplayer/"></a><br />
At this point you should be able to check that Flash Player is properly installed and see it in action:</p>
<p><a class="lightbox" title="stage-video-debug" href="https://blogsimages.adobe.com/osmf/files/2011/02/stage-video-debug.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-156" title="stage-video-debug" src="https://blogsimages.adobe.com/osmf/files/2011/02/stage-video-debug.png" alt="" width="661" height="814" srcset="https://blogsimages.adobe.com/osmf/files/2011/02/stage-video-debug.png 661w, https://blogsimages.adobe.com/osmf/files/2011/02/stage-video-debug-243x300.png 243w" sizes="(max-width: 661px) 100vw, 661px" /></a></p>
<p>You can see the performance boost that Stage Video brings by comparing the CPU levels in a player that uses Stage Video against a player that doesn’t. To make this kind of comparison easy, we exposed the enableStageVideo property as a configuration setting in Strobe Media Playback. When set to false, the Video object is used, even if Stage Video is available:<br />
<a href="http://osmf.org/dev/latest/debug.html?src=rtmp://cp67126.edgefcs.net/ondemand/mp4:mediapm/osmf/content/test/sample1_700kbps.f4v&amp;logFilter=videoClasses&amp;enableStageVideo=false">http://osmf.org/dev/latest/debug.html?logFilter=videoClasses&amp;enableStageVideo=false</a></p>
<h2>Check the Stage Video workflow implementation in your OSMF-based video player</h2>
<p>Now you are probably eager to test the performance of your video player with the exploratory implementation of Stage Video support in OSMF.</p>
<p>For this you’ll need to get the Flex SDK that contains the latest Stage Video APIs, you’ll need our source, and you’ll need to set up your development environment.</p>
<ol>
<li>Get the source code from our Sourceforge SVN space: svn checkout http://svn.code.sf.net/adobe/smp/svn/trunk</li>
<li>Import the OSMF and StrobeMediaPlayback projects.</li>
<li>Download the Flex Hero SDK (<a href="http://opensource.adobe.com/wiki/display/flexsdk/Download+Flex+Hero">Flex 4.5 SDK – build 4.5.0.19786</a>+) here: <a href="http://opensource.adobe.com/wiki/display/flexsdk/Download+Flex+Hero">http://opensource.adobe.com/wiki/display/flexsdk/Download+Flex+Hero</a></li>
<li>Add the new Flex SDK to Flash Builder (right click on any project, Properties, ActionScript Compiler, Configure Flex SDKs). Set the new SDK as the default.</li>
<li>Check that you can run the StrobeMediaPlayback project without any issues.</li>
<li>Link your video player to the OSMF project that was imported in step 2.</li>
<li>Go to the compiler properties (right click on it, Properties, ActionScript Compiler) and set “Use specific version” to 10.2.</li>
<li>In additional compiler arguments add   -swf-version=11</li>
<li>Go to your HTML page and add wmode=direct to your parameters. Your embed snippet should look like this:</li>
</ol>
<p>swfobject.embedSWF(<br />
&#8220;StrobeMediaPlayback.swf&#8221;<br />
, &#8220;StrobeMediaPlayback&#8221;<br />
, 640<br />
, 480<br />
, &#8220;10.1.0&#8221;<br />
, &#8220;expressInstall.swf&#8221;<br />
, parameters<br />
, {<br />
allowFullScreen: &#8220;true&#8221;,<br />
<strong> wmode: &#8220;direct&#8221;</strong><br />
},<br />
,{<br />
name: &#8220;StrobeMediaPlayback&#8221;<br />
}<br />
);</p>
<p>At this point you are ready to run your video player.</p>
<p>Normally, your video player should behave exactly as it did before switching to the exploratory version of OSMF.</p>
<h2>Troubleshooting</h2>
<p>If the video is not visible, check to see if you have an opaque DisplayObject in your display list that may be covering the Stage Video. To fix this issue, you can make it transparent, for example. This was the only issue that we had to fix in Strobe Media Playback before we got seamless video playback and a great performance boost!</p>
