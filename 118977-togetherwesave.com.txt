<div class="wpb_gallery_slides wpb_flexslider flexslider_slide flexslider" data-interval="10" data-flex_fx="slide"><ul class="slides"><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Clean refrigerator coils annually.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>When buying a new computer, select an ENERGY STAR® model.  Consider buying a laptop as it uses less energy than a comparable desktop. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Keep fireplace dampers closed unless a fire is burning.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>When heating or cooling, keep windows shut and locked.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use microwave for cooking when possible.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>If you must do smaller loads, adjust the water level in the washing machine to match the load size, especially when using hot water.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure outside soffit vents are not blocked.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure drapes and shades are open during the day to catch free solar heat in winter.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure refrigerator door seals are tight.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Enable auto power down feature on gaming consoles. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Don’t let the water run while you are shaving or brushing your teeth.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use refrigerator’s anti-sweat feature only if necessary.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>If not frost-free, regularly defrost refrigerator or freezer to avoid ice buildup.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>If rinsing dirty dishes before putting them into the dishwasher, do so with cold water. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use cold water for garbage disposal.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure insulation in your attic does not block soffit vents.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Verify dryer vent hose is tightly connected to the inside wall fitting.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure your outdoor heat pump/air conditioning unit is kept clean and free of debris.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Do not close off unused rooms that are conditioned by forced-air systems.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Plant trees and shrubs to provide shade on the east, south and west sides of your home. Evergreen trees and shrubs can provide a windbreak on the north side.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Caulk around plumbing penetrations that come through walls beneath bathroom and kitchen sinks.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use outdoor security lights with a photocell and/or a motion sensor.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Run ceiling paddle fans on medium, blowing down in summer and paddle fans on low, blowing up in winter.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Set thermostats to 78 degrees Fahrenheit in summer, 68 degrees Fahrenheit in winter.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Only do full laundry loads.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Close shades and drapes during the day to help keep heat out in summer.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure floor registers are not blocked with rugs, drapes or furniture.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure electric blankets are turned off in the morning.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use bath towels at least twice before washing them.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Caulk electrical wire penetrations at the top of the interior walls in the attic.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Replace outdoor lighting with its equivalent outdoor-rated LED bulb. LEDs work well in cold weather. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Switch your refrigerator’s power-saver feature to “ON,” if available.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn off pool pumps and heaters when not needed.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Drain 1-2 gallons from bottom of water heater each year to reduce sediment build up.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn computers and monitors off when not in use.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>When cooking on the oven range, use pot lids to help food cook faster. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn large-screen TVs off completely when not in use.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Clean dryer’s lint trap before each load.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>If you are heating water on the stove, use hot tap water instead of cold.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Limit shower length to 5-7 minutes.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Only run dishwasher when fully loaded. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Insulate exposed hot water lines.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>For households with 1 or 2 members, a 115 degree Fahrenheit setting may work fine. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Have your HVAC system serviced once per year by a NATE-certified technician.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>When using the fireplace, turn down your heating system thermostat.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Remove and clean window A/C filter monthly.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Do not close supply air registers.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>When using the fireplace, open the outside air vent (if provided) or open the nearest window slightly.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Check that the dryer vent hose is tightly connected to dryer.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Plug electronic devices such as cable boxes, printers and TVs into power strips to turn off during vacations or long periods without use.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn off bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans 15 minutes after the job is complete or install 15 minute timers on bathroom ventilator fans.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use heavy-duty, clear sheets of plastic sealed tightly on the inside of windows to reduce the amount of cold air entering your home during the winter.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Check for energy saving settings on flat-panel TVs like automatic brightness control and a power saving sleep mode. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure windows and doors are properly weather-stripped and use door sweeps.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Minimize use of electric space heaters, except for limited or temporary spot heating.  Turn space heaters off when leaving the room.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Close shades and drapes at night to keep heat in during the winter.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn off stereos and radios when not in use.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Caulk along baseboards with a clear sealant.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Replace any light bulb, especially ones that are on more than one hour per day, with a light-emitting diode (LED) bulb. &nbsp;</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Request an ENERGY STAR® set-top box from cable or satellite provider.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Outside your home, caulk around all penetrations including telephone, electrical, cable, gas, water spigots, dryer vents, etc.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Keep your garage door down.  A warmer garage in the winter and cooler garage in the summer will save energy.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Caulk around storm windows and basement windows.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Dry consecutive loads to harvest heat remaining in dryer from last load.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Fix dripping faucets.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn off unnecessary lighting.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Always use cold-water rinse.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Unplug unused refrigerators or freezers. Recycle them if you do not need them.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Monitor your home’s relative humidity in the summer.  If it consistently stays in the 60 percent range or higher, ask your HVAC technician about lowering your central air conditioning unit’s indoor fan speed.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Change HVAC air filters monthly.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Unplug battery chargers when not needed.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Wash clothes in cold water. Use hot water only for very dirty loads.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Install a programmable thermostat to save even more.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use dishwasher’s air-dry cycle instead of the heat-dry cycle to dry dishes.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Install water saving shower heads.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure window A/C units are weather-stripped. Remove the unit in the winter and close and lock the window.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure dryer vent hose is not kinked or clogged.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Remember to use the kitchen exhaust fan when cooking.  Turn it off after cooking.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>When installing new air filters, make sure they are facing in the correct direction (look for arrow on side of filter).</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure attic access door closes tightly and is insulated.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use fixtures with electronic ballasts and T-8, 32 Watt fluorescent lamps.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Minimize clothes drying time by using an auto moisture sensor, if available.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure the dryer’s outdoor exhaust door is not blocked or clogged.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Set the refrigerator temperature to 34 &#8211; 37 degrees Fahrenheit and freezer temperature to 0 &#8211; 5 degrees Fahrenheit.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Consider using a “solar-powered” clothes dryer: an old fashioned clothes line.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn off coffee makers when not in use.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Check to be sure return air grilles are not blocked by furniture or bookcases.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Install heat traps on hot and cold water lines when it’s time to replace your water heater.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Ensure all new appliances, electronics and lights are ENERGY STAR® labeled.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Use a slow-cooker instead of simmering foods on the stove.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Install a water heater wrap, also known as a water heater blanket, per manufacturer’s instructions.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Turn off ceiling fans when leaving the room. Fans cool people, not rooms.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Make sure heat tape is off when not needed.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Set water heater temperature no higher than 120 degrees Fahrenheit.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>If you don’t unplug them, use energy-saving modes or automatic sleep functions on electronics.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Seal electric wall outlets and wall switches with foam pads.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Do not use roof-top power ventilators for attic exhaust as they may draw conditioned air from your home.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Verify livestock water tank heaters are off when not needed.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>Keep “fresh-air” vents on window A/C units closed.</p>
</div></li><li><div class="flex-caption"><p>In hot weather, avoid running the dryer during the heat of the day.</p>

$ = jQuery;

var greedyButton = document.createElement('button');
var greedyDiv = document.createElement('div');
var greedyHide = document.createElement('ul');

greedyDiv.className = 'hamburger';
greedyHide.className = 'hidden-links hidden';
$('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu ul').addClass("visible-links");

var greedyMark = $(greedyButton).append(greedyDiv);
$(greedyMark).insertBefore('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu ul');
$(greedyHide).insertAfter('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu ul');

var $nav = $('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu');
var $btn = $('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu button');
var $vlinks = $('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu .visible-links');
var $hlinks = $('.tse-sub-panel-menu .tse-sub-menu .hidden-links');
var $gsearch = $('#topnav-search').addClass('visible');
$btn.addClass('hidden');

var breaks = [];

function updateNav() {
  
  var availableSpace = $btn.hasClass('hidden') ? $nav.width() : $nav.width() - $btn.width() - 30;

  // The visible list is overflowing the nav
  if($vlinks.width() > availableSpace) {

    // Record the width of the list
    breaks.push($vlinks.width());

    // Move item to the hidden list
    $vlinks.children().last().prependTo($hlinks);

    // Show the dropdown btn
    if($btn.hasClass('hidden')) {
      $btn.removeClass('hidden');
      $gsearch.addClass('hidden');
    }

  // The visible list is not overflowing
  } else {

    // There is space for another item in the nav
    if(availableSpace > breaks[breaks.length-1]) {

      // Move the item to the visible list
      $hlinks.children().first().appendTo($vlinks);
      breaks.pop();
    }

    // Hide the dropdown btn if hidden list is empty
    if(breaks.length < 1) {
      $btn.addClass('hidden');
      $hlinks.addClass('hidden');
      $gsearch.removeClass('hidden');
    }
  }

  // Keep counter updated
  $btn.attr("count", breaks.length);

  // Recur if the visible list is still overflowing the nav
  if($vlinks.width() > availableSpace) {
    updateNav();
  }

}

// Window listeners

$(window).resize(function() {
    updateNav();
});

$btn.on('hover', function() {
  $hlinks.toggleClass('hidden');
});
$btn.on('click', function() {
  $hlinks.toggleClass('hidden');
});
$hlinks.on('mouseleave', function() {
  $hlinks.toggleClass('hidden');
});
updateNav();

</script>
