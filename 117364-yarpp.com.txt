	<h2 id="description-header">Description</h2>
	<p>Yet Another Related Posts Plugin (YARPP) displays pages, posts, and custom post types related to the current entry, introducing your readers to other relevant content on your site.</p>
<p><strong>YARPP Basic for Standard Features</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Thumbnail or list view</strong> of related content.</li>
<li><strong>Related posts, pages, and custom post types.</strong></li>
<li><strong>Templating</strong>: The YARPP templating system gives you advanced control of how your results are displayed.</li>
<li><strong>An advanced and versatile algorithm</strong>: Using a customizable algorithm considering post titles, content, tags, categories, and custom taxonomies, YARPP finds related content from across your site. <a href="http://wordpress.tv/2011/01/29/michael-%E2%80%9Cmitcho%E2%80%9D-erlewine-the-yet-another-related-posts-plugin-algorithm-explained/" rel="nofollow">Learn More.</a>  </li>
<li><strong>Related posts in feeds</strong>: Display related posts in RSS feeds with custom display options.</li>
</ul>
<p>This plugin requires PHP 5, MySQL 4.1, and WordPress 3.3 or greater. See <a href="https://wordpress.org/plugins/yet-another-related-posts-plugin/faq/">the FAQ</a> for answers to common questions.</p></div>

	<h2 id="installation-header">Installation</h2>
	<h4>Auto display on your website</h4>
<ol>
<li>Copy the folder <code>yet-another-related-posts-plugin</code> into the directory <code>wp-content/plugins/</code> and activate the plugin.</li>
<li>(optionally) copy the sample template files inside the <code>yarpp-templates</code> folder into your active theme.</li>
<li>Go to the &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; settings page to customize YARPP.</li>
</ol>
<h4>Auto display in your feeds</h4>
<p>Make sure the &#8220;display related posts in feeds&#8221; option is turned on if you would like to show related posts in your RSS and Atom feeds. The &#8220;display related posts in feeds&#8221; option can be used regardless of whether you auto display them on your website (and vice versa).</p>
<h4>Widget</h4>
<p>Related posts can also be displayed as a widget. Go to the Appearance &gt; Widgets options page and add the &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; widget. Choose to display content from YARPP Basic. The widget will only be displayed on single entry (permalink) pages.</p>
<p>The YARPP Basic widget can be used even if the &#8220;auto display&#8221; option is turned off.</p>
<h4>Custom display through templates</h4>
<p>YARPP allows the advanced user with knowledge of PHP to customize the display of related posts using a templating mechanism.</p></div>

<div
	id="faq"
	class="plugin-faq section "
>
	<h2 id="faq-header">FAQ</h2>
	<p><strong>Common Questions about YARPP Basic</strong></p>
<p>Below are Frequently Asked Questions about YARPP basic.</p>
<p>If your question isn&#8217;t here, ask your own question at <a href="https://wordpress.org/support/plugin/yet-another-related-posts-plugin" rel="nofollow">the WordPress.org forums</a>.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Many pages list &#8220;no related posts.&#8221;</dt>
<dd>
<p>Most likely you have &#8220;no related posts&#8221; right now because the default &#8220;match threshold&#8221; is too high. Here&#8217;s what I recommend to find an appropriate match threshold: lower your match threshold in the YARPP &#8220;Relatedness&#8221; options to something very low, like 1. (If you don&#8217;t see the match threshold, you may need to display the &#8220;Relatedness&#8221; options via the &#8220;Screen Options&#8221; tab at the top.) Most likely the really low threshold will pull up many posts that aren&#8217;t actually related (false positives), so look at some of your posts&#8217; related posts and their match scores. This will help you find an appropriate threshold. You want it lower than what you have now, but high enough so it doesn&#8217;t have many false positives.</p>
</dd>
<dt>How can I move the related posts display?</dt>
<dd>
<p>If you do not want to show the Related Posts display in its default position (right below the post content), first go to YARPP options and turn off the &#8220;automatically display&#8221; options in the &#8220;website&#8221; section. If you would like to instead display it in your sidebar and you have a widget-aware theme, YARPP provides a Related Posts widget which you can add under &#8220;Appearance&#8221; &gt; &#8220;Widgets.&#8221;</p>
<p>If you would like to add the Related Posts display elsewhere, edit your relevant theme file (most likely something like <code>single.php</code>) and add the PHP code <code>related_posts();</code> within <a href="https://codex.wordpress.org/The_Loop" rel="nofollow">The Loop</a> where you want to display the related posts. (Make sure you don&#8217;t add <code>echo related_posts();</code> or you may end up with duplicates in your related posts section.)</p>
</dd>
<dt>How can I limit related posts to a certain time frame? For instance, I don&#8217;t want to show posts from two years ago.</dt>
<dd>
<p>Yes. In WordPress, go to &#8220;Settings&#8221; and &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; and make sure &#8220;The Pool&#8221; is checked in the &#8220;Screen Options&#8221; section at the top of the page.  In &#8220;The Pool&#8221; section, check the box next to &#8220;Show only posts from the past <em>X</em> months.&#8221;</p>
</dd>
<dt>Where do I tell YARPP to display related posts only by tags?</dt>
<dd>
<p>In WordPress, go to &#8220;Settings&#8221; and &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; and make sure &#8220;Relatedness&#8221; is checked in the &#8220;Screen Options&#8221; section at the top of the page.  In the &#8220;Relatedness&#8221; section, configure the dropdown boxes next to &#8220;Titles,&#8221; &#8220;Bodies,&#8221; &#8220;Categories,&#8221; and &#8220;Tags.&#8221;</p>
</dd>
<dt>Can I specify related posts?</dt>
<dd>
<p>Sorry, but specifying related posts, displaying related posts from external WordPress sites, and pulling content from the Comments section are all outside the scope of YARPP at this time.</p>
</dd>
<dt>I&#8217;m seeing related posts displayed on the home page. How do I prevent that?</dt>
<dd>
<p>Some WordPress themes treat the home page as an archive or a &#8220;page.&#8221; Go to &#8220;Settings&#8221; then &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; and view the &#8220;Display Options&#8221; section. Make sure &#8220;Pages&#8221; and &#8220;Also display in archives&#8221; are not checked.</p>
</dd>
<dt>How can I prevent the &#8220;related posts&#8221; list from displaying on specific posts?</dt>
<dd>
<p>If you have several posts where you don&#8217;t want to display related posts and they all share a similar category or tag, you could use &#8220;Disallow by Category&#8221; or &#8220;Disallow by Tag&#8221; in &#8220;The Pool&#8221; section. (Go to &#8220;Settings&#8221; and &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; and make sure &#8220;The Pool&#8221; is checked in the &#8220;Screen Options&#8221; section at the top of the page.)</p>
<p>You could also add <code>&lt;!--noyarpp--&gt;</code> to the HTML code of any post to prevent related posts from displaying. This solution will only work if you are using &#8220;Automatic Display&#8221; in the &#8220;Display Options&#8221; section. If you are programatically calling <code>related_posts()</code> from PHP code, you&#8217;ll have to do your own checking to see if related posts are appropriate to display or not.</p>
</dd>
<dt>I&#8217;m using the Thumbnails display in YARPP 4. How do I override the style of the text that displays? The title only shows two lines, the font is larger than I&#8217;d like, I&#8217;d like to center the thumbnails, etc.</dt>
<dd>
<p>If you&#8217;re familiar with CSS, you can override any YARPP styles by editing your theme&#8217;s <code>style.css</code> file, or any other CSS file you may have created that loads after the YARPP one. To edit your theme&#8217;s CSS file, go to &#8220;Appearance&#8221; then &#8220;Editor&#8221; and then click <code>style.css</code> in the right sidebar. Add changes at the bottom of the file and click &#8220;Save.&#8221; If you do edit this file, just make sure you add <code>!important</code> after each style declaration, to make sure they&#8217;ll override the YARPP rules.</p>
<p>Some common overrides that YARPP users have added are:</p>
<pre><code>/* Reduces the title font size and displays more than two title lines */
.yarpp-thumbnail {height: 200px !important;}
.yarpp-thumbnail-title {font-size:0.8em !important; max-height: 4em !important}

/* Centers the thumbnail section */
.yarpp-related-widget {text-align:center !important;}
</code></pre>
<p>Once you save any CSS changes, empty your browser&#8217;s cache and reload your page to see the effect.</p>
</dd>
<dt>I&#8217;m using the Thumbnails display. How can I change the thumbnail size?</dt>
<dd>
<p>The default YARPP thumbnail size is 120px by 120px. The thumbnail size can be specified programmatically by adding <code>add_image_size('yarpp-thumbnail', $width, $height, true);</code> to your theme&#8217;s <code>functions.php</code> file with appropriate width and height variables. In the future I may add some UI to the settings to also set this. Feedback is requested on whether this is a good idea.</p>
<p>Each time you change YARPP&#8217;s thumbnail dimensions like this, you will probably want to have WordPress regenerate appropriate sized thumbnails for all of your images. I highly recommend the <a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/regenerate-thumbnails/" rel="nofollow">Regenerate Thumbnails</a> plugin for this purpose.</p>
</dd>
<dt>I&#8217;m using the Thumbnails display. Why aren&#8217;t the right size thumbnails being served?</dt>
<dd>
<p>By default, if an appropriately sized thumbnail is not available in WordPress, a larger image will be served and will be made to fit in the thumbnail space via CSS. Sometimes this means images will be scaled down in a weird way, so it is not ideal. What you really want is for YARPP to serve appropriately-sized thumbnails.</p>
<p>There are two options for doing so:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>First, you can use the <a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/regenerate-thumbnails/" rel="nofollow">Regenerate Thumbnails</a> plugin to generate all these thumbnail-sized images in a batch process. This puts you in control of when this resizing process happens on your server (which is good because it can be processor-intensive). New images which are uploaded to WordPress should automatically get the appropriate thumbnail generated when the image is uploaded.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Second, you can turn on a feature in YARPP to auto-generate appropriate size thumbnails on the fly, if they have not yet been created. Doing this type of processing on the fly does not scale well, so this feature is turned off by default. But if you run a smaller site with less traffic, it may work for you. Simply add <code>define('YARPP_GENERATE_THUMBNAILS', true);</code> to your theme&#8217;s <code>functions.php</code> file.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</dd>
<dt>I&#8217;m using the Thumbnails display. Why are some of my posts missing appropriate images?</dt>
<dd>
<p>YARPP&#8217;s thumbnail view requires that a WordPress &#8220;featured image&#8221; be set for each post. If you have many posts that never had a featured image set, I recommend the plugin <a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/auto-post-thumbnail/" rel="nofollow">Auto Post Thumbnail</a>, which will generate post thumbnails for you.</p>
</dd>
<dt>How can I use the custom template feature?</dt>
<dd>
<p>YARPP&#8217;s custom templates feature allows you to uber-customize the related posts display using the same coding conventions and <a href="https://codex.wordpress.org/Template_Tags" rel="nofollow">Template Tags</a> as in WordPress themes. Custom templates must be in your <em>active theme&#8217;s main directory</em> in order to be recognized by YARPP. If your theme did not ship with YARPP templates, move the files in the <code>yarpp-templates</code> directory which ships with YARPP into your active theme&#8217;s main directory. Be sure to move the <em>files</em> (which must be named <code>yarpp-template-</code>&#8230;<code>.php</code>) to your theme, not the entire directory.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Is YARPP compatible with WordPress Multisite?</dt>
<dd>
<p>YARPP should work fine in a multisite environment, and many users are running it without any issues using WordPress Multisite. It will, however, only get results <em>within</em> each blog. It will not display related posts results from across your network.</p>
</dd>
<dt>I want to use YARPP on a site with content in multiple languages.</dt>
<dd>
<p>The recommended solution in such cases is to use the <a href="https://polylang.wordpress.com/" rel="nofollow">Polylang</a> plugin. Polylang has posted <a href="https://polylang.wordpress.com/2013/05/03/polylang-and-yarpp/" rel="nofollow">a tutorial for using YARPP with Polylang</a>.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Does YARPP work with full-width characters or languages that don&#8217;t use spaces between words?</dt>
<dd>
<p>YARPP works fine with full-width (double-byte) characters, assuming your WordPress database is set up with Unicode support. 99% of the time, if you&#8217;re able to write blog posts with full-width characters and they&#8217;re displayed correctly, YARPP will work on your blog.</p>
<p>However, YARPP does have difficulty with languages that don&#8217;t place spaces between words (Chinese, Japanese, etc.). For these languages, the &#8220;consider body&#8221; and &#8220;consider titles&#8221; options in the &#8220;Relatedness options&#8221; may not be very helpful. Using only tags and categories may work better for these languages.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Does YARPP slow down my blog/server?</dt>
<dd>
<p>The YARPP calculation of related content does make a little impact, yes. However, YARPP caches all of its results, so any post&#8217;s results need only be calculated once. YARPP&#8217;s queries have been significantly optimized since version 3.5.</p>
<p>If you are running a large site and need to throttle YARPP&#8217;s computation, try the official <a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/yarpp-experiments/" rel="nofollow">YARPP Experiments</a> plugin which adds this throttling functionality. If you are looking for a hosting provider whose databases will not balk under YARPP, I personally have had great success with <a href="http://www.mediatemple.net/#a_aid=4ed59d7ac5dae" rel="nofollow">MediaTemple</a>.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Are there any plugins that are incompatible with YARPP?</dt>
<dd>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/disqus-comment-system/" rel="nofollow">DISQUS</a>: go to the DISQUS plugin advanced settings and turn on the &#8220;Check this if you have a problem with comment counts not showing on permalinks&#8221;.</li>
<li><a href="https://wordpress.org/support/topic/267966" rel="nofollow">SEO_Pager plugin</a>: turn off the automatic display option in SEO Pager and instead add the code manually.</li>
<li><a href="http://www.elektroelch.de/hacks/wp/pagebar/" rel="nofollow">Pagebar 2</a>;</li>
<li><a href="https://wordpress.org/support/topic/392605" rel="nofollow">WP Contact Form III plugin and Contact Form Plugin</a>;</li>
<li><a href="http://wpml.org" rel="nofollow">WPML</a>: various incompatibilities have been reported. The multilingual plugin <a href="https://polylang.wordpress.com/" rel="nofollow">Polylang</a> has great support for YARPP and is suggested as a replacement for WPML.</li>
<li>Other related posts plugins, obviously, may also be incompatible.</li>
</ul>
<p>Please submit similar bugs by starting a new thread on <a href="https://wordpress.org/support/plugin/yet-another-related-posts-plugin" rel="nofollow">the WordPress.org forums</a>. I check the forums regularly and will try to release a quick bugfix.</p>
</dd>
<dt>YARPP seems to be broken since I upgraded to WordPress X.X.</dt>
<dd>
<p>Before upgrading to a new WordPress version, you should first deactivate all plugins, then upgrade your WordPress, and then reactivate your plugins. Even then, you may still find that something went wrong with your YARPP functionality. If so, try these steps:</p>
<ol>
<li>Visit the &#8220;Related Posts (YARPP)&#8221; settings page to verify your settings.</li>
<li>Deactivate YARPP, replace the YARPP files on the server with a fresh copy of the new version, and then reactivate it.</li>
<li>Install the official <a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/yarpp-experiments/" rel="nofollow">YARPP Experiments</a> plugin to flush the cache.</li>
</ol>
</dd>
<dt>Can I clear my cache? Can I build up the cache manually?</dt>
<dd>
<p>The official <a href="https://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/yarpp-experiments/" rel="nofollow">YARPP Experiments</a> plugin adds manual cache controls, letting you flush the cache and build it up manually.</p>
</dd>
<dt>I removed the YARPP plugin but I still see YARPP-related database tables. Shouldn&#8217;t those be removed, too?</dt>
<dd>
<p>Beginning with version 4.0.7, YARPP includes clean uninstall functionality. If you no longer wish to use YARPP, first deactivate YARPP using the &#8220;Plugins&#8221; page in WordPress, then click the &#8220;Delete&#8221; link found on the same page. This process will automatically remove all YARPP-related files, including temp tables. If you manually try to remove YARPP files instead of going through WordPress, some files or temp tables could remain.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Does YARPP support custom post types?</dt>
<dd>
<p>Yes. To make YARPP support your custom post type, the attribute <code>yarpp_support</code> must be set to true on the custom post type when it is registered. It will then be available on options on the YARPP settings page.</p>
<pre><code>'yarpp_support' =&gt; true
</code></pre>
<p>If you would like to programmatically control which post types are considered in an automatically-displayed related posts display, use the <code>yarpp_map_post_types</code> filter.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Can I customize how YARPP displays?</dt>
<dd>
<p>Yes. Developers can call YARPP&#8217;s powerful relatedness algorithm from anywhere in their own code. Some examples and more details are in my slides from my <a href="http://www.slideshare.net/mitcho/relate-all-the-things" rel="nofollow">WordCamp Birmingham talk</a>.</p>
<pre><code>yarpp_related(array(
    // Pool options: these determine the "pool" of entities which are considered
    'post_type' =&gt; array('post', 'page', ...),
    'show_pass_post' =&gt; false, // show password-protected posts
    'past_only' =&gt; false, // show only posts which were published before the reference post
    'exclude' =&gt; array(), // a list of term_taxonomy_ids. entities with any of these terms will be excluded from consideration.
    'recent' =&gt; false, // to limit to entries published recently, set to something like '15 day', '20 week', or '12 month'.
    // Relatedness options: these determine how "relatedness" is computed
    // Weights are used to construct the "match score" between candidates and the reference post
    'weight' =&gt; array(
        'body' =&gt; 1,
        'title' =&gt; 2, // larger weights mean this criteria will be weighted more heavily
        'tax' =&gt; array(
            'post_tag' =&gt; 1,
            ... // put any taxonomies you want to consider here with their weights
        )
    ),
    // Specify taxonomies and a number here to require that a certain number be shared:
    'require_tax' =&gt; array(
        'post_tag' =&gt; 1 // for example, this requires all results to have at least one 'post_tag' in common.
    ),
    // The threshold which must be met by the "match score"
    'threshold' =&gt; 5,

    // Display options:
    'template' =&gt; , // either the name of a file in your active theme or the boolean false to use the builtin template
    'limit' =&gt; 5, // maximum number of results
    'order' =&gt; 'score DESC'
),
$reference_ID, // second argument: (optional) the post ID. If not included, it will use the current post.
true); // third argument: (optional) true to echo the HTML block; false to return it
</code></pre>
<p>Options which are not specified will default to those specified in the YARPP settings page. Additionally, if you are using the built-in template rather than specifying a custom template file in <code>template</code>, the following arguments can be used to override the various parts of the builtin template: <code>before_title</code>, <code>after_title</code>, <code>before_post</code>, <code>after_post</code>, <code>before_related</code>, <code>after_related</code>, <code>no_results</code>, <code>excerpt_length</code>.</p>
<p>If you need to use related entries programmatically or to know whether they exist, you can use the functions <code>yarpp_get_related( $args, $reference_ID )</code>  and  <code>yarpp_related_exist( $args, $reference_ID )</code>. <code>yarpp_get_related</code> returns an array of <code>post</code> objects, just like the WordPress function <code>get_posts</code>. <code>yarpp_related_exist</code> returns a boolean for whether any such related entries exist. For each function, <code>$args</code> takes the same arguments as those shown for <code>yarpp_related</code> above, except for the various display and template options.</p>
<p>Note that custom YARPP queries using the functions mentioned here are <em>not</em> cached in the built-in YARPP caching system. Thus, if you notice any performance hits, you may need to write your own code to cache the results.</p>
</dd>
<dt>Does YARPP support custom taxonomies?</dt>
<dd>
<p>Yes. Any taxonomy, including custom taxonomies, may be specified in the <code>weight</code> or <code>require_tax</code> arguments in a custom display as above. <code>term_taxonomy_id</code> specified in the <code>exclude</code> argument may be of any taxonomy.</p>
<p>If you would like to choose custom taxonomies to choose in the YARPP settings UI, either to exclude certain terms or to consider them in the relatedness formula via the UI, the taxonomy must (a) have either the <code>show_ui</code> or <code>yarpp_support</code> attribute set to true and (b) must apply to either the post types <code>post</code> or <code>page</code> or both.</p>
</dd>

We have been made aware that this change infringed upon the WordPress guidelines. We apologize for the issue and have remedied the situation in update 4.0.8. Going forward, we would really appreciate your input to help us continue to improve the product. We are primarily looking for country, domain, and date installed information. Please help us make YARPP better by opting in to this information and by filling out our quick, <a href="http://www.surveymonkey.com/s/Z278L88" rel="nofollow">5 question survey</a>. Thank you.</li>
