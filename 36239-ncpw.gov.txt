    <h2 id="get-involved">Get Involved</h2>
<div class="field field-name-field-os-collection-desc field-type-text-long field-label-hidden">
  
                <p>You can be a part of NCPW! Check out these tips for spreading the word, reaching local press and social media – plus tools like social media images and a sample article to personalize and promote NCPW, or your own community event.</p>
<h3>Reach out to your community!</h3>
<ul><li><strong>Plan an event</strong> - Host a forum, workshop or seminar in your community and share the tools people need for today's economy. Partner with local organizations such as a police department or library.</li>
<li><strong>Write about it</strong> - Draft an article and send it to local organizations so they can promote consumer education through their publications.</li>
<li><strong>Blog about it</strong> - Add a comment about NCPW to blogs you regularly read. Keep your comment brief and include a link to your NCPW event, your website for consumer tips, or <a href="http://www.consumer.ftc.gov">consumer.ftc.gov</a>.</li>
<li><strong>Spread the word</strong> - Encourage your audience to tell friends, family, colleagues and neighbors about NCPW. Word of mouth is powerful!</li>
</ul><h3>Resources for local press and social media outreach</h3>
<p>Contact your local television, radio or cable access station. Offer to talk about a consumer protection topic you care about, or tell their audience about your NCPW events. Use the following materials to let them know about your organizations’ involvement in NCPW: </p>
