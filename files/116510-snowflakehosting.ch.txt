<h1>snowflake Ops: Welcome<a class="headerlink" href="#snowflake-ops-welcome" title="Permalink to this headline">¶</a></h1>
<p>This is the snowflake Ops documentation.
It&#8217;s targeted at an technical audience, mainly developers and sysadmins.
Marketing and contract related details are available on our <a class="reference external" href="https://snowflakeops.ch">website</a>.</p>
<div class="section" id="version-5-generation-201501">
<h2>Version 5 (Generation 201501)<a class="headerlink" href="#version-5-generation-201501" title="Permalink to this headline">¶</a></h2>
<p>We are using so called server generations,
based on a certain Debian release and tied to versions of further software like PHP, MySQL and so on.
This concept allows you to select the appropriate version depending on the application you use,
and also to switch to a newer generation in a planned way according to your needs.
Right now, you are looking at the documentation for the version 5:</p>

<div class="section" id="open-source">
<h2>Open source<a class="headerlink" href="#open-source" title="Permalink to this headline">¶</a></h2>
<p>We strongly believe in open source software.
While this documentation is released as open soure already (<a class="reference external" href="https://github.com/snowflakeOps/docs">GitHub Project</a>),
we are working hard to publish our Puppet wrapper modules as well.
Checkout our <a class="reference external" href="https://github.com/snowflakeOps">GitHub Organisation</a> or <a class="reference external" href="https://twitter.com/snowflakeOps">Twitter</a> for updates.</p>
