<div id="about-gardasil" class="isi-section">
<div class="title-text">Information About <span>GARDASIL 9</span></div>
<div class="textarea"><span class="highlighted-text">GARDASIL<sup>®</sup>9</span> (Human Papillomavirus 9-valent Vaccine, Recombinant) helps protect girls and women ages 9 to 26 against cervical, vaginal, vulvar, and anal cancers and genital warts caused by 9 types of HPV.</div>
<div class="textarea"><span class="highlighted-text">GARDASIL 9</span> helps protect boys and men ages 9 to 26 against anal cancer and genital warts caused by those same HPV types.</div>
<div class="textarea"><span class="highlighted-text">GARDASIL 9</span> may not fully protect everyone, nor will it protect against diseases caused by other HPV types or against diseases not caused by HPV.</div>
<div class="textarea"><span class="highlighted-text">GARDASIL 9</span> does not prevent all types of cervical cancer, so it’s important for women to get routine cervical cancer screenings.</div>
<div class="textarea"><span class="highlighted-text">GARDASIL 9</span> is a shot that is usually given in the arm muscle. <span class="highlighted-text">GARDASIL 9</span> may be given as 2 or 3 shots.</div>
<ul class="mobile-only">
<li><span>For persons 9 through 14 years of age, <span class="highlighted-text noWrap">GARDASIL 9</span> can be given using a <span class="noWrap">2-dose</span> or <span class="noWrap">3-dose</span> schedule. For the <span class="noWrap">2-dose</span> schedule, the second shot should be given 6–12 months after the first shot. If the second shot is given less than 5 months after the first shot, a third shot should be given at least <span class="noWrap">4 months</span> after the second shot. For the <span class="noWrap">3-dose</span> schedule, the second shot should be given 2 months after the first shot and the third shot should be given 6 months after the first shot.</span></li>
<li><span>For persons 15 through 26 years of age, <span class="highlighted-text noWrap">GARDASIL 9</span> is given using a <span class="noWrap">3-dose</span> schedule; the second shot should be given 2 months after the first shot and the third shot should be given 6 months after the first shot.</span></li>
</ul>
<div class="textarea">The appropriate dosing schedule will be determined by your child’s health care professional.</div>

<section id="importantSafetyInfo" class="importantSafetyInfo">
<h2>IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION</h2>
<section class="innerPara">Anyone who is allergic to the ingredients of <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> or GARDASIL, including those severely allergic to yeast, should not receive the vaccine. <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> was not studied in women who knew they were pregnant.</section>
<p>The side effects include:</p>
<ul class="content-list">
<li><span>Pain</span></li>
<li><span>Swelling</span></li>
<li><span>Redness</span></li>
<li><span>Itching</span></li>
<li><span>Bruising</span></li>
<li><span>Bleeding</span></li>
<li><span>A lump where your child got the shot</span></li>
<li><span>Headache</span></li>
<li><span>Fever</span></li>
<li><span>Nausea</span></li>
<li><span>Dizziness</span></li>
</ul>
<section class="innerPara">Fainting can happen after getting <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9.</span> Sometimes people who faint can fall and hurt themselves. For this reason, your health care professional may ask your child to sit or lie down for 15 minutes after your child gets <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span>. Some people who faint might shake or become stiff.</section>
<section class="innerPara">Only a doctor or health care professional can decide if <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> is right for your child.</section>

<section class="importantSafetyGardasil mobile-only">
<h2 class="saf-two-c">INFORMATION ABOUT <span class="gardasil9Italic">GARDASIL 9</span></h2>

<section><span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL<sup>®</sup>9</span> <span class="gardasil-generictext">(Human Papillomavirus 9-valent Vaccine, Recombinant)</span> helps protect girls and women ages 9 to 26 against cervical, vaginal, vulvar, and anal cancers and genital warts caused by 9 types of HPV. <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> helps protect boys and men ages 9 to 26 against anal cancer and genital warts caused by those same HPV types.</section>
<section><span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> may not fully protect everyone, nor will it protect against diseases caused by other HPV types or against diseases not caused by HPV.</section>
<section><span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> does not prevent all types of cervical cancer, so it’s important for women to continue routine cervical cancer screenings. <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> does not treat cancer or genital warts.</section>
<section><span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> is a shot that is usually given in the arm muscle. <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> may be given as 2 or 3 shots.</section>
</section>
<ul class="content-list for_person mobile-only">
<li><span>For persons 9 through 14 years of age, <span class="gardasil-text noWrap">GARDASIL 9</span> can be given using a <span class="noWrap">2-dose</span> or <span class="noWrap">3-dose</span> schedule. For the <span class="noWrap">2-dose</span> schedule, the second shot should be given 6–12 months after the first shot. If the second shot is given less than 5 months after the first shot, a third shot should be given at least <span class="noWrap">4 months</span> after the second shot. For the <span class="noWrap">3-dose</span> schedule, the second shot should be given 2 months after the first shot and the third shot should be given 6 months after the first shot.</span></li>
<li><span>For persons 15 through 26 years of age, <span class="gardasil-text">GARDASIL 9</span> is given using a <span class="noWrap">3-dose</span> schedule; the second shot should be given 2 months after the first shot and the third shot should be given 6 months after the first shot.</span></li>
</ul>
<section class="mobile-only">The appropriate dosing schedule will be determined by your child’s health care professional.</section>
<section class="paraLast">You are encouraged to report negative side effects of prescription drugs to the FDA. Visit <a href="//www.fda.gov/medwatch" target="_blank" class="external_link">www.fda.gov/medwatch</a> or call 1-800-FDA-1088.</section>
<section class="paraLast">Please read the <a href="http://www.merck.com/product/usa/pi_circulars/g/gardasil_9/gardasil_9_ppi.pdf" target="_blank">Patient Information</a> for <span class="gardasil-text gardasil-text2">GARDASIL 9</span> and discuss it with your child’s doctor. The physician <a href="http://www.merck.com/product/usa/pi_circulars/g/gardasil_9/gardasil_9_pi.pdf" target="_blank">Prescribing Information</a> also is available.</section>
