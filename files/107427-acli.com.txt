<h2 style="margin-left: 15px;">Where ACLI Stands</h2>
</div>
<blockquote class="hero-unify line-height-130">
<p>
<strong><a href="/Public-Policy/Fiduciary-Rule">Fiduciary</a></strong>  As currently written, the fiduciary regulation significantly harms consumers’ ability to plan and save for financially secure retirements. ACLI supports reasonable and appropriately tailored rules that require all sales professionals to act in their customers' best interest. <a href="https://www.acli.com/Public-Policy/Fiduciary-Rule" target="_blank">[Read more.]</a>
</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote class="hero-unify line-height-130">
<p>
<strong><a href="/Public-Policy/FSOC-Reform">Financial Stability Oversight Council (FSOC) Reform</a></strong>   ACLI believes that FSOC’s authority to designate nonbank financial companies as systemically significant should be repealed. FSOC has used this authority inconsistently and with a lack of transparency. <a href="/Public-Policy/FSOC-Reform">[Read more.]</a> </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote class="hero-unify line-height-130">
<p>
<strong><a href="/Public-Policy/Retirement-Plans">Retirement Plans</a></strong>   ACLI supports the private retirement system, which provides a robust and growing foundation for millions of Americans’ retirement security through defined contribution plans, such as 401(k)s, as well as IRAs and individual annuities. <a href="/Public-Policy/Retirement-Plans">[Read more.]</a>
</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote class="hero-unify line-height-130">
<p>
<strong><a href="/Public-Policy/Taxes">Taxes</a></strong>   As Congress and state legislatures consider approaches to tax reform, policy-makers must carefully consider the negative effects on families if changes to public policy make it more expensive to obtain financial and retirement security. <a href="/Public-Policy/Taxes">[Read more.]</a>
</p>
</blockquote>
