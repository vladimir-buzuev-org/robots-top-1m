            <div class="isi-content">

                

<p class="text-bold mobileIsiFont" style="margin-bottom:5px;">
    NAMZARIC is a prescription medicine approved to treat moderate to severe Alzheimer’s 
    disease in patients who are taking donepezil hydrochloride 10&nbsp;mg, the active ingredient in Aricept<sup>®</sup>.
</p>


                

<p style="margin-bottom:5px;">
    <strong>There is no evidence that NAMZARIC prevents or slows the underlying disease process in patients with Alzheimer's disease.</strong>
</p>


                <div class="h3 text-uppercase first" id="IRI">
                    Important Risk Information
                </div>
                

<p>
    <span class="text-bold">NAMZARIC should not be taken by anyone who:</span> has an allergy to memantine 
    HCl, donepezil HCl, medicines that contain piperidines, or any of the ingredients in NAMZARIC.
</p>

<p class="text-bold">
    Before taking NAMZARIC, tell the doctor about all of the patient’s medical conditions, including:
</p>
<ul class="normal">
    <li>heart problems including an irregular, slow, or fast heartbeat
    </li>
    <li>asthma or lung problems
    </li>
    <li>seizures
    </li>
    <li>stomach ulcers
    </li>
    <li>bladder, kidney, or liver problems
    </li>
    <li>any surgical, dental, or other medical procedures scheduled when anesthesia may be used
    </li>
</ul>

<p>
   Tell the doctor about all the medicines the patient takes, including prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and herbal supplements.
</p>

<div class="h4">
    What are the possible side effects of NAMZARIC?
</div>

<p class="text-bold">
    NAMZARIC may cause serious side effects, including:
</p>
<ul class="normal">
    <li>muscle problems in patients given anesthesia
    </li>
    <li>slow heartbeat and fainting. This happens more often in people with heart problems. Call the doctor right away if the patient faints while taking NAMZARIC.
    </li>
    <li>more stomach acid. This raises the chance of ulcers and bleeding especially when taking NAMZARIC. The risk is higher for patients who have had ulcers, or take aspirin or other NSAIDs.
    </li>
    <li>nausea and vomiting
    </li>
    <li>difficulty passing urine
    </li>
    <li>seizures
