The new extradosed bridge will connect Zambia and Botswana, shortening transit times from 36 to merely 2 hours. <br /><br />DSI has secured a supply contract with the Korean company BNG Consultants, one of the leading bridge construction companies in Korea, for the new Kazungula Bridge. From 2016 to 2017, DSI will supply material and equipment to the jobsite and will support BNG Consultants with testing and supervision services. <br /><br />DSI is supplying the DYNA Grip<sup>®</sup> Stay Cable System including DYNA<sup>®</sup> Link Anchor Boxes. The Kazungula Bridge is the first application of DYNA<sup>®</sup> Link in Africa. On the whole, DSI will supply 332t of stay cable strand for the 923m long bridge, which features 6 pylons. The required DYNA Grip<sup>®</sup> Stay Cable sizes are DG-P31, DG-P37 and DG-P55.<br /><br />The supply contract with BNG Consultants was signed in Munich by Mr. Keun-Taek Kim (President and CEO of BNG), Mr. Wlodkowski (DSI RCEO EMEA Construction) and Mr. Kahl (DSI Special Project Manager EMEA). Mr. Kim had travelled to Munich together with Mr. Jong-Eun Park, BNG’s Project Manager and Mr. Shin-Chan Jang, DSI Korea. <br /><br />Mr. Wlodkowski and Mr. Kahl invited their special guests on a sightseeing tour of Munich and a visit to the Hofbraeuhaus to toast to the successful signing of the contract.</p></div></div></div></div></div>
