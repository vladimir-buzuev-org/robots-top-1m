                <header>
                    <h2><a href="https://yahoo.tumblr.com/post/160514740259/introducing-yahoos-neurodiversity-employee">Introducing &nbsp;Yahoo&rsquo;s Neurodiversity Employee Resource Group</a></h2>
                </header>
                <section>
                    <p><i>By Margenett Moore-Roberts, Global Head of Inclusive Diversity</i>&nbsp;</p><p>I am excited to announce the creation of Yahoo&rsquo;s newest Employee Resource Group (ERG) focused on Neurodiversity, spearheaded by Margaux Joffe, Yahoo&rsquo;s Head of Production, Global Marketing Department. The ERG&rsquo;s mission is to champion neurodiversity at Yahoo in order to create an inclusive workplace for minds of all kinds.<b><br></b></p><figure data-orig-width="1980" data-orig-height="1020" class="tmblr-full"><img src="https://68.media.tumblr.com/522a5edfb082078c52e7a6a61e676886/tumblr_inline_opqq39YLkL1qhxx5s_500.jpg" alt="image" data-orig-width="1980" data-orig-height="1020" width="500" height="257"></figure><p>If you&rsquo;re unfamiliar, neurodiversity refers to differences in learning and attention as normal variations of the human brain. At Yahoo, we recognize minds of all kinds and respect all forms of human variations &ndash; from dyslexia, to ADHD, to the autistic spectrum and more. In partnership with Yahoo&rsquo;s Office of Inclusive Diversity (OID), the new Neurodiversity ERG will help encourage that anyone with these differences can utilize their strengths at work and thrive through one another&rsquo;s support, as well as supports OID&rsquo;s mission of advancing diversity and inclusion in Yahoo&rsquo;s workforce, workplace and marketplace.</p><p>Margaux is the chair of the program and her passion stems from her personal experience in being diagnosed with ADHD three years ago, when she was well into adulthood. She felt compelled to found the <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.kaleidoscopesociety.com&amp;t=ZDQxMjU5MzI4NjRkODNkMjhhZWZkNDNhNjZiMTBlOGQ4M2FkYjgyYiw0alNoSnphMg%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F160514740259%2Fintroducing-yahoos-neurodiversity-employee&amp;m=0">Kaleidoscope Society</a>, an empowerment project for women with ADHD which shines a light on inspiring stories of trailblazing women with ADHD for its more than 700 members. After joining Yahoo and talking to the OID about their commitment to creating an inclusive working environment, she proposed the idea of creating a Neurodiversity ERG in order to extend the OID&rsquo;s efforts to colleagues (like herself) with neurological differences.</p><p>The ERG is hitting the ground running, and the first internal campaign is introducing poster series and screens in all offices globally to highlight the strengths associated with a few neurological differences such as Autism, ADHD, Dyslexia and OCD. The ERG will also meet regularly to provide support to relevant members of the Yahoo community and collaborate on how to grow and bring awareness to their mission. Four initial focuses of the program include: learning the needs of employees, creating a community of support, providing resources at Yahoo and increasing the cultural competency of managers so they can effectively manage their diverse teams. </p><p>In the coming months, the ERG will work with the OID and Human Resources to ensure Neurodiversity is incorporated into our Inclusion strategy and our manager competencies.</p>

            <article class="text featured   reblog" id="post_159942040324" data-postID="159942040324" data-permalink="https://yahoo.tumblr.com/post/159942040324/researching-the-definition-of-good-online" data-reblog="https://www.tumblr.com/reblog/159942040324/MNT970wR">
                <header>
                    <h2><a href="https://yahoo.tumblr.com/post/159942040324/researching-the-definition-of-good-online">Researching the Definition of Good Online Conversations and How They Should Rank with the Yahoo News Annotated Comments Corpus</a></h2>
                </header>
                <section>
                    <p><a href="https://yahooresearch.tumblr.com/post/159942008511/researching-the-definition-of-good-online" class="tumblr_blog">yahooresearch</a>:</p><blockquote><p>By Courtney Napoles, <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fresearch.yahoo.com%2Fresearchers%2Faasishkp&amp;t=Zjk3MTZhMTM5OGE3YWVkODVlZTkxNDBkNzY2ZmIwMjNkNGVlNGIxMixNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">Aasish Pappu</a>, Joel Tetreault<b><br></b></p><p>Comment threads following online news articles often range from vacuous to hateful. That said, good conversations <i>do</i> occur online, with people expressing different viewpoints and attempting to inform, convince, or better understand the other side, even if they can get lost among the multitude of unconstructive comments. At Yahoo Research, we show in recent statistical experiments that automatically identifying and <i>ranking good</i> conversations on top will cultivate a more civil and constructive atmosphere in online communities and potentially encourage participation from more users [1]. </p><p>In an effort to foster more respectful online discussions and encourage more research among academics surrounding comments, we present the <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fwebscope.sandbox.yahoo.com%2Fcatalog.php%3Fdatatype%3Dl%26did%3D83&amp;t=Zjk5MjhjY2EwODUyZDliMWUwZDk4ZTE1Mzg1MDg1OTIyZTU3Y2NmNyxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">Yahoo News Annotated Comments Corpus</a> (YNACC) via our data sharing program, <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwebscope.sandbox.yahoo.com%2F&amp;t=NGIyNTk0YTZiNTRlZDY5ZmIyNjlmMzdjNDZmMWQ5MjY1ZTdlMzYxOCxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">Webscope</a>. The corpus contains 522K comments from 140K comment threads posted in response to online news articles, and contains manual annotations for a subset of 2.4K comment threads and 9.2K comments. The annotations include 6 attributes of individual comments: sentiment, tone, agreement with other commenters, topic of the comment, intended audience, and persuasiveness. The annotations also include 3 attributes of threads: constructiveness, agreeability within the conversation, and type of conversation, i.e., <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FFlaming_%28Internet%29&amp;t=ZmRiMmI0YTU4MWU0OWFiMGUyN2U5M2MxMjdjYzRkOGM0NGMzYjY4OSxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">flamewars</a> vs positive/respectful [2].</p><p>Annotated conversations in the YNACC corpus were used to create a predictive algorithm and train statistical models to automatically detect &ldquo;good&rdquo; conversations. We call these good conversations ERICs: Engaging, Respectful, and/or Informative Conversations, and they are characterized by:<br></p><ul><li>A respectful exchange of ideas, opinions, and/or information in response to a given topic or topics.<br></li></ul><ul><li>Opinions expressed as an attempt to elicit a dialogue or persuade.<br></li></ul><ul><li>Comments that seek to contribute some new information or perspective on the relevant topic.</li></ul><p><a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fs.yimg.com%2Fge%2Fynacc%2FYNACC01.png&amp;t=M2ZkOTQ5MDA1ZWU3NzFhZWY3MGYwMWVmNmRjM2Y0YWNhNDhkYzljNyxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0"></a></p><a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fs.yimg.com%2Fge%2Fynacc%2FYNACC01.png&amp;t=M2ZkOTQ5MDA1ZWU3NzFhZWY3MGYwMWVmNmRjM2Y0YWNhNDhkYzljNyxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0"><img src="https://s.yimg.com/ge/ynacc/YNACC01.png" alt="image"></a><p><a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fs.yimg.com%2Fge%2Fynacc%2FYNACC02.png&amp;t=MTQzNDE3ZTBlOWQwYzZmZDUwYzljMDM0MjM3OWE2MGM5MDA1OTdlOSxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0"></a></p><a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fs.yimg.com%2Fge%2Fynacc%2FYNACC02.png&amp;t=MTQzNDE3ZTBlOWQwYzZmZDUwYzljMDM0MjM3OWE2MGM5MDA1OTdlOSxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0"><img src="https://s.yimg.com/ge/ynacc/YNACC02.png" alt="image"></a><center>Example of an ERIC conversation (top) and a non-ERIC conversation (bottom)</center><br><p>ERICs have no single identifying attribute. A good conversation is determined by how many respectful, engaging, and persuading comments are present. For instance, an exchange where communicants are in total agreement throughout can be an ERIC, as can an exchange with a heated disagreement. Our algorithm ranks either of these types of exchanges higher than those that lack ERICs. Many of the labels for the ERICs in our dataset are the result of a new coding scheme (annotation taxonomy) we developed and are for characteristics of online conversations not captured by traditional argumentation or dialogue features. Some of the labels we collected have been annotated in previous work [3,4], and this is the first time they are aggregated in a single corpus at the dialogue level.</p><p>Additionally, we collected annotations on 1K threads from the <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.lrec-conf.org%2Fproceedings%2Flrec2016%2Fpdf%2F1126_Paper.pdf&amp;t=ODA2NDE2MzdkOTYyZGMwZGNhYmFlYmZlMDIxMzU4NjM5MjM5ZTk4OSxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">Internet Argument Corpus</a>, representing another domain of online debates. Our corpus and annotation scheme is the first exploration of how characteristics of individual comments contribute to the dialogue-level classification of an exchange. We hope YNACC will facilitate research to understand ERICs and other aspects of dialogue in general. </p><p><b>The technical contributions of this dataset are described in two scientific papers:</b></p><p>[1] Courtney Napoles, Aasish Pappu, and Joel Tetreault. <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fresearch.yahoo.com%2Fpublications%2F8882%2Fautomatically-identifying-good-conversations-online-yes-they-do-exist&amp;t=MThhNTYwYmFlZDU5MTA0ZDI0YjIzYjBjN2MzYzZlMDUzZjRiYzFjNCxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">&ldquo;Automatically Identifying Good Conversations Online (Yes, they do exist!)&rdquo;</a>. In Proceedings of ICWSM'17.</p><p>[2] Courtney Napoles, Joel Tetreault, Aasish Pappu, Enrica Rosato and Brian Provenzale. 2017. <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fresearch.yahoo.com%2Fpublications%2F8881%2Ffinding-good-conversations-online-yahoo-news-annotated-comments-corpus&amp;t=YTE0NTA4YTk2YWY0YzBlODE0YzRhOWQwZTlhY2FiYzc3OWU0NmI0MyxNTlQ5NzB3Ug%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159942040324%2Fresearching-the-definition-of-good-online&amp;m=0">&ldquo;Finding Good Conversations Online: The Yahoo News Annotated Comments Corpus&rdquo;</a>. In Proceedings of The 11th Linguistic Annotation Workshop (LAW-XI).</p><p><b>References:</b></p><p>[3] Rob Abbott, Brian Ecker, Pranav Anand, and Marilyn Walker. Internet Argument Corpus 2.0: An SQL schema for dialogic social media and the corpora to go with it. LREC 2016.</p><p>[4] Marilyn Walker, Jean Fox Tree, Pranav Anand, Rob Abbott, and Joseph King. A corpus for research on deliberation and debate. LREC 2012.<br></p></blockquote>

            <article class="text featured   yahoo yahoo_news yahoo_finance editorial weed family weed_and_the_american_family marist marist_poll" id="post_159675841809" data-postID="159675841809" data-permalink="https://yahoo.tumblr.com/post/159675841809/yahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american" data-reblog="https://www.tumblr.com/reblog/159675841809/xyHtUBJM">
                <header>
                    <h2><a href="https://yahoo.tumblr.com/post/159675841809/yahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american">Yahoo News Investigation | Weed &amp; the American Family</a></h2>
                </header>
                <section>
                    <p><i>By Martha Nelson, Global Editor in Chief</i></p><p>The topic of marijuana is at a tipping point. While some aspects of this tectonic shift in American society have been well covered &ndash; legalization, the business boom, the political divides &ndash; its impact on our most important relationships, those inside our homes, has been curiously underexamined.<b><br></b></p><p>Leveraging the power of the Yahoo network, <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.yahoo.com%2Fweed&amp;t=NGQwZGMwMDNjNDA0NjU5NjQ3Y2RjYzJhM2FiMTliNjE5NjRkZjYyNSx4eUh0VUJKTQ%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159675841809%2Fyahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american&amp;m=0">Weed &amp; the American Family</a> is an in-depth and thoughtful examination of the way cannabis is affecting our families &ndash; from the generational split over usage, to shifting attitudes, to its impact on parenting and relationships. &nbsp;&nbsp;</p><figure class="tmblr-embed tmblr-full"><iframe width="500" height="281" src="https://tumblr.yahoo.com/video/weed-american-family-intro-090000403.html?format=embed" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></figure><p>At the center of our project is an exclusive Yahoo News/Marist Poll which offers the most comprehensive examination of this hot topic &ndash; Weed &amp; the American Family.<br></p><p>This survey of 1,122 adults in America found that marijuana use has become surprisingly acceptable, particularly in families where parents are users. Having used &ndash; or merely tried &ndash; marijuana makes you much less concerned about its effect on your family, suggesting that as weed becomes more widespread in the age of legalization, more Americans will begin to feel the same way. &nbsp;</p><p>See the full poll results here: <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=http%3A%2F%2Fmaristpoll.marist.edu%2Fyahoo-newsmarist-poll%2F&amp;t=MTAzZGJmZWZmZWQzNjNiM2Q2M2NjNTg1MWFmZDRjYjNjZTgzMjc3ZSx4eUh0VUJKTQ%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159675841809%2Fyahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american&amp;m=0">http://maristpoll.marist.edu/yahoo-newsmarist-poll/</a></p><figure data-orig-width="600" data-orig-height="1835" class="tmblr-full"><img src="https://68.media.tumblr.com/706620d261398cebd921129a6d2a338f/tumblr_inline_oojyq30thd1qhxx5s_1280.jpg" alt="image" data-orig-width="600" data-orig-height="1835" width="500" height="1529"></figure><p>Our examination includes an interview with former <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.yahoo.com%2Fnews%2Fkennedy-medicinal-marijuana-trojan-horse-recreational-drug-industry-094751917.html&amp;t=N2U1MTYxMjFhMGM5Y2U3NTMyZmJlOGExNjJmZjQ4ZDhjZjUxMmMyYix4eUh0VUJKTQ%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159675841809%2Fyahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american&amp;m=0">U.S. Rep. Patrick Kennedy</a>, who co-founded an anti-legalization group called Smart Approaches to Marijuana (SAM); a discussion with <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.yahoo.com%2Fnews%2Fcolorados-governor-john-hickenlooper-warily-learns-live-pot-092311376.html&amp;t=NzRjYzI2ZWE0ZjM1ZmQzZWVhNTE4ZDJiMDA5NjVmOGFjZGM5ZDUxZCx4eUh0VUJKTQ%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159675841809%2Fyahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american&amp;m=0">Colorado Gov. John Hickenlooper</a> about the lessons he&rsquo;s learned as a reluctant leader on the front lines of the marijuana revolution; and an intimate conversation with musician, mother and weed advocate <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.yahoo.com%2Fnews%2Fcannabis-advocate-melissa-etheridge-id-much-rather-smoke-grown-kids-drink-091952114.html&amp;t=N2IwNTdkNjQ4YWUxZjUwOWYxMjhmMTczOWU1YWFiMGJlMzEzNjM0Zix4eUh0VUJKTQ%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159675841809%2Fyahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american&amp;m=0">Melissa Etheridge</a>. We also produced video diaries of families who share how marijuana has affected their lives, both negatively and positively, including a compelling account of <a href="http://t.umblr.com/redirect?z=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.yahoo.com%2Fnews%2Fcannabis-made-better-parent-one-moms-confession-095418707.html&amp;t=MTk5Y2YxMWU1MGI4NzVjMGViZjM4NTYxMjhlMDQ2OGVjMzFhY2ZjNSx4eUh0VUJKTQ%3D%3D&amp;b=t%3AobUI-xcktBB4E04prKaEgw&amp;p=https%3A%2F%2Fyahoo.tumblr.com%2Fpost%2F159675841809%2Fyahoo-news-investigation-weed-the-american&amp;m=0">a mother using marijuana to treat postpartum depression and anxiety</a>.<br></p><p>We hope that as you read through our fascinating poll data, reporting and first-person accounts on marijuana use, you&rsquo;ll be able to develop a better sense of the complex and evolving attitudes about &nbsp;this deeply controversial issue in American life and that it generates discussion where it matters most &ndash; &nbsp;within your own family. &nbsp;<br></p>
