                          <div class="NavMoreInfoWrapper"><h2>About EAA</h2>
<br />

                        </div>

                                <p><p>Paul Poberezny came from humble beginnings, yet he emerged as one of the 20th century's greatest aviation leaders, creating a worldwide aviation organization and the world's largest annual fly-in event, EAA AirVenture in Oshkosh, Wisconsin.</p></p>

<div id="eaaCommunity">
    <h1>
        Young Eagles®</h1>
    <div class="desc">

      <center>
<p><img alt="YE 25th Anniversary" src="/~/media/images/eaa/educationresources/youngeagles/ye-25th-anniversary-logo-240.jpg?la=en&amp;h=150&amp;w=150" style="height: 150px; width: 150px;" />&nbsp;<a href="http://www.phillips66aviation.com/#/Base"><img style="height: 99px; width: 269px;" alt="Young Eagles Presenting Sponsor Phillips 66" src="/~/media/images/eaa/educationresources/youngeagles/middle_column_phillips.gif?la=en&amp;h=99&amp;w=269" /></a></p>
</center>
<p>Founded in 1992, the Young Eagles program has dedicated nearly 25 years to giving youth ages 8&ndash;17 their first free ride in an airplane.</p>
<p>It&rsquo;s the only program of its kind, with the sole mission to introduce and inspire kids in the world of aviation. </p>
<p>Today, the Young Eagles program has flown over 2 million kids with the help of EAA&rsquo;s network of volunteer pilots and ground volunteers.&nbsp;</p>
<p>We reached our goal and celebrated the 2 millionth Young Eagle flown during AirVenture Oshkosh 2016.&nbsp;Join us in offering every child, tween, and teen the chance to experience the Spirit of Aviation<sup>TM</sup>.</p>
