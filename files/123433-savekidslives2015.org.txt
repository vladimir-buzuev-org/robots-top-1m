It adopted the Shanghai Declaration on Health Promotion which focused on the delivery of the SDG agenda on health, emphasising links with urbanisation. The conference held between 21-24 November also ratified the Shanghai Healthy Cities Mayors' Consensus which includes emphasis on the need for safe and sustainable mobility in urban centres.</p>
<p>Speaking on the opening day of the conference, global road safety advocate and UNDP Goodwill Ambassador for the SDGs Michelle Yeoh said that protecting children on the world’s roads must be addressed as a priority. <div class="media_embed mceNonEditable youtube " style="width: 560px; height: 320px;"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/VZiAzIvxvuY" width="560" height="315" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen=""></iframe></div>
<p> </p>
<p>The report, launched at Habitat III, surveys the progress towards safer walking and cycling infrastructure in over 20 low- to middle-income countries across Africa, Asia and Latin America, where compared with high-income countries, twice as many more people die in road traffic crashes. Of the countries included in the report, four African countries were the most dangerous to walk and cycle. In Malawi, a total of 66 per cent of all road fatalities were pedestrians and cyclists; in Kenya 61 per cent; South Africa 53 per cent; Zambia 49 per cent; and in Nepal 49 per cent.</p>
<p>The report provides detailed summaries for each country which include key non-motorised transport commitments and policy goals or policy statements. <p><br>Motorized transport is responsible for a quarter of global carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions and is the fastest growing sector in greenhouse gas emissions. <p>Erik Solheim, Executive Director of UN Environment, said: “In Nairobi alone, 500 pedestrians are dying each year. This is a staggering number. Designing transport systems around cars puts more vehicles on the road, increasing both greenhouse gas emissions and deadly air pollution. We must put people first in transport systems.”</p>
<p><strong>Share the Road’s report urges countries to:</strong></p>
<ul><li>Draft national and local policies for Non-motorized Transport (NMT), and if they already exist, immediately act to implement them.</li>
<li>Increase spending on walking and cycling infrastructure to at least 20 per cent of transport budgets.</li>
<li>Ask NMT users where they walk and ride – pay particular focus to vulnerable users, such as women, children, elderly and people with mobility challenges.</li>
<li>Actively champion NMT – political will is not only about policies, it is about giving walking and cycling equal status to private cars.<br><br></li>

							<h3 class="news__title"><a class="news__title-link i-invert-underline" href="#news-walk-to-school-day-a-vision-zero-for-kids-usa" data-toggle="modal">Walk to School Day - a ‘Vision Zero for Kids', USA</a></h3>
							<span class="news__date">October 12, 2016</span>
							<div class="news__text">
								<a href="#news-walk-to-school-day-a-vision-zero-for-kids-usa" data-toggle="modal"><img alt="Walk to School Day - a ‘Vision Zero for Kids', USA" class="news__image" src="images/resized/180/120/uploads/nieuws/us_vision_zero_e.jpg" /></a>

												<h3 class="modal-title" id="news-label-walk-to-school-day-a-vision-zero-for-kids-usa">Walk to School Day - a ‘Vision Zero for Kids', USA</h3>
											</div>
											<div class="modal-body">
												<img class="news__image news__image--modal" src="images/resized/280/190/uploads/nieuws/us_vision_zero_e.jpg" alt="Walk to School Day - a ‘Vision Zero for Kids', USA" />
												<p style="text-align:left;">At Walk to School Day in Washington, DC, the FIA Foundation and the US National Center for Safe Routes to School have announced a new youth-focused partnership to tackle traffic fatalities.<br><br></p>
<p style="text-align:center;"><br><img src="webbeheer/html/phpThumb/phpThumb.php?src=/uploads/nieuws/walk_to_school_logo.jpg&amp;w=499&amp;h=333&amp;q=95" alt="walk to school logo"><br><br></p>
<p>The International <a href="https://www.transportation.gov/fastlane/celebrating-20th-walk-school-day" target="_blank">Walk to School Day</a>, is a global event bringing together communities from over 40 countries, walking and cycling to school on the same day. In the US, thousands of schools from each state celebrated, reaching record-breaking participation among the 50,000 events held since the initiative began. In the nation’s capital, DC Mayor Muriel Bowser joined students from fourteen schools for the 20th anniversary of Walk to School Day.<br><br></p>
<p style="text-align:center;"><img style="margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;" src="webbeheer/html/phpThumb/phpThumb.php?src=/uploads/nieuws/us_vision_zero_main.jpg&amp;w=498&amp;h=332&amp;q=95" alt="us vision zero main"></p>
<p style="text-align:left;">The event also kicked off a partnership between the FIA Foundation and the National Center for Safe Routes to School to work with cities to implement Vision Zero for Kids. “Together, we’re pioneering an effort to support cities in focusing on youth to reach zero fatalities. We know that when we improve conditions for children, we’re making it safer for all ages and abilities,” said Nancy Pullen-Suefert, Director of the National Center for Safe Routes to School.<br><br> The partnership also includes the USDOT Federal Highway Administration. The initiative has received guidance from the National Association of City Transportation Officials, the National League of Cities, the Vision Zero Network, and cities with strong pedestrian programs including Los Angeles, Miami-Dade County, New York City, Philadelphia, San Francisco, Seattle and Washington, DC. The partnership supports the work of the <a href="http://www.childhealthinitiative.org/" target="_blank">Global Initiative for Child Health and Mobility</a>, with the goal of a safe and healthy journey to school for all children by 2030.<br><br>DC Mayor Muriel Bowser supported the initiative by signing a commitment to a future with zero traffic deaths and a focus on youth. Many communities are pursuing this strategy to implement Vision Zero and save lives on our roads. Such efforts represent key goals of USDOT’s <a href="https://www.transportation.gov/mayors-challenge" target="_blank">Mayors’ Challenge for Safer People and Safer Streets</a>.<br><br></p>
<p style="text-align:center;"><img src="webbeheer/html/phpThumb/phpThumb.php?src=/uploads/nieuws/us_vision_zero_e.jpg&amp;w=499&amp;h=333&amp;q=95" alt="us vision zero e"><br><strong>Youth support #SaveKidsLives</strong></p>
<p style="text-align:left;"><br>US Transportation Secretary Foxx, a former mayor, recognized the unique role of city leaders in promoting pedestrian and bicycle safety. “We are proud to support Walk to School Day and help communities as they work to eliminate traffic fatalities and serious injuries.”<br><br>The goal to reach zero deaths was also echoed in the Road to Zero Coalition hosted by the US Department of Transportation on the same day. The Coalition strives to end fatalities on American roads within the next 30 years and includes USDOT, Federal Highway Administration, Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration, and the National Safety Council. <br><br><strong>This goal reflects the urgency to reduce road fatalities in the US, which suffered its largest increase of fatalities last year since 1966 and an even higher toll in the first half of 2016. On a global level, it is a step towards the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 3.6, to halve the number of deaths and injuries on the roads by 2020.</strong><br><br>The Coalition focuses on the safe systems approach, which is the foundation of Vision Zero. Commitment to supporting a safe systems approach and a safe and healthy journey to school is included in a major United Nations document that the US will adopt later this month.</p>
<p style="text-align:left;"><br>Along with other UN member states, the US is expected to sign the UN Habitat III New Urban Agenda, a roadmap for sustainable urbanization. “This document recognizes what we’re celebrating here today – that schools are the heartbeat of a community. A safe systems approach will help us save lives in our schools, our country, and our world,” said Natalie Draisin, US Manager.<br><br><a href="http://www.saferoutesinfo.org/" target="_blank">Learn more about the US National Center for Safe Routes to School here</a></p>
