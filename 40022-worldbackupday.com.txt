  <section id="how">
    <div class="container">
      <article>
        <h3>So how do I backup?</h3>
        
        <p>Most people backup their files in one of two ways: to an external drive, or somewhere on the Internet.</p>

<p>It&#8217;s really easy, and you only need to set it up once!</p>

<aside><strong>Disclaimer:</strong> Even though this process is super simple, we&#8217;re not responsible for any data loss. Make sure you read through each screen carefully and you should be fine!</aside>

        <details>
          <summary><h4>Backing up to a USB Drive</h4></summary>
        
        
          <p>You&#8217;ll need an <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=sr_st_popularity-rank?keywords=external+hard+drive&amp;qid=1395785089&amp;rh=n%3A172282%2Cn%3A541966%2Cn%3A1292110011%2Cn%3A595048%2Ck%3Aexternal+hard+drive&amp;sort=popularity-rank">external hard drive</a> for this, preferably one that holds more than your computer can.</p>

<p>The good news is, they&#8217;re cheap!</p>
                
          <details>
            <summary>Windows 7</summary>
          
            <ol>
<li>Click the <strong>Start button</strong>.</li>
<li>Type <strong>&#8220;backup&#8221;</strong> into the search box.</li>
<li>Click on <strong>Backup and Restore</strong>.</li>
<li>Choose <strong>&#8220;Set up Backup&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>When Windows has finished preparing, select your external hard drive and click Next.</li>
<li>Click <strong>&#8220;Let Windows Choose&#8221;</strong> and press Next.</li>
</ol>

<aside>You can change when Windows backs up your files by clicking &#8220;Change Backup Schedule&#8221; if you like. The more often, the better!</aside>

<p>Then click <strong>&#8220;Save settings and run backup&#8221;</strong>. </p>

<p>You&#8217;re all done! Windows will backup <strong>automatically</strong> from now on. Make sure you leave your hard drive plugged in, or if you&#8217;re on a laptop, set a reminder to plug it in often.</p>

<p>Congrats on setting up your first backup!</p>
          </details>
          <details>
            <summary>Windows 8</summary>
            
            <ol>
<li>Open your start screen and start typing <strong>&#8220;file history settings&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>In the search results panel, tap or click <strong>&#8220;File History Settings&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Tap or click <strong>&#8220;Select a drive&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Choose your external hard drive from the list.</li>
<li>Click <strong>&#8220;Turn on&#8221;</strong>.</li>
</ol>

<p>That was easy. Windows will backup all your files <strong>automatically</strong> from now on. Make sure you leave your hard drive plugged in - or at least plug it in often.</p>

<p>Congratulations on setting up your first backup!</p>
          </details>
        
          <details>
            <summary>Mac</summary>
            
            <p>First, plug in your USB hard drive. Then, follow the instructions below to start automatically backing up your Mac!</p>

<aside>**WARNING:** Make sure your new USB drive is totally empty! Time Machine will usually *erase the drive* so it can do its thing. If this is a new drive and you&#8217;re okay with it, keep reading!</aside>

<ol>
<li>Open the &#63743; menu (in the top left of your screen) and choose <strong>System Preferences</strong>.</li>
<li>Click on <strong>&#8220;Time Machine&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Click <strong>&#8220;Select Disk&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Choose your USB hard drive from the list and select <strong>&#8220;Use Disk&#8221;</strong>.</li>
</ol>

<p>You&#8217;re done! Your Mac will back itself up <strong>automatically</strong> from now on. If you&#8217;re using a MacBook, make sure to plug in your hard drive regularly so it can save your files!</p>
          </details>
          
          <details>
            <summary>Linux</summary>
            
            <p>Every Linux distribution offers various tools to backup your data. We&#8217;ve posted the instructions for Ubuntu below.</p>

<ol>
<li>Open the HUD (click the Ubuntu button in the Dash) and type <strong>&#8220;Backup&#8221;</strong>. </li>
<li>Click on the search result <strong>&#8220;Backup&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Click on <strong>&#8220;Just show my backup settings&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Activate <strong>&#8220;Automatic backups&#8221;</strong>.</li>
<li>Choose the location to store your backup on the tab <strong>&#8220;Storage</strong>&#8221; (your external drive).</li>
<li>Choose how often to backup on the tab <strong>&#8220;Schedule&#8221;</strong>. The more often, the <br />
better!</li>
<li>Click on <strong>&#8220;Back Up Now&#8221;</strong> on the tab <strong>&#8220;Overview&#8221;</strong> to start backing up. </li>
</ol>

<p>That was easy. Ubuntu will backup all your files <strong>automatically</strong> from <br />
now on. Make sure you leave your hard drive plugged in - or at least <br />
plug it in often. </p>

<p>Congratulations on setting up your first backup!</p>
          </details>
                  
        </details>
        
        <details>
          <summary><h4>Backing up Online</h4></summary>
          <p>A simple way to start saving your files to the internet is by using <a href="http://skydrive.com">OneDrive</a>, <a href="http://dropbox.com">Dropbox</a>, <a href="http://drive.google.com">Google Drive</a>, or even <a href="https://www.amazon.com/clouddrive/home/">Amazon Cloud Drive</a> to backup the most important things you&#8217;re working on right now. </p>

<p>These &#8220;online drives&#8221; usually don&#8217;t have too much space, and only backup one folder on your computer. However, this is great for students and businesspeople who need to keep their most important documents safe.</p>

<h5>Backing up your whole computer</h5>

<p>If you&#8217;d rather back up absolutely EVERYTHING (good on you!) - just search for &#8220;online backup reviews&#8221; on your favorite search engine. It can help you choose the service that&#8217;s right for you.</p>
        </details>
