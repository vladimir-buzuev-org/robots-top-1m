            <h1>Browser Support</h1>
            
        </section>
        

<div class="markLinks">
    <p>
 If you are seeing this page, it is likely that a white bar is present at the top of Australian JobSearch which has the text &quot;Your experience could be hindered by your browser. Click here for update info.&quot; If not, this content may not be relevant to you.</p>
<p>
 &nbsp;</p>
<h2>
 What does this mean?</h2>
<p>
 Australian JobSearch is optimised for the Internet Explorer 9-11, Mozilla Firefox, Google Chrome and Safari web browsers.</p>
<p>
 Older versions of Internet Explorer, such as Internet Explorer 6, 7 and 8, may not be able to display the site as well as newer browsers.</p>
<p>
 In some cases, Internet Explorer&nbsp;9 or greater will also display the browser message, if compatibility view is turned on. This web site works best with compatibility view turned off.</p>
<p>
 &nbsp;</p>
<h2>
 How do I get rid of the message &quot;Your experience could be hindered by your browser&quot;?</h2>
<h3>
 Internet Explorer&nbsp;9, 10, 11&nbsp;users</h3>
<p>
 In the Internet Explorer &quot;Tools&quot; menu, please un-tick the &quot;Compatibility View&quot; option. This tells Internet Explorer that JobSearch supports the latest web standards and should improve the appearance of the web site.<br />
 &nbsp;</p>
<h3>
 Internet Explorer 6, 7, 8&nbsp;users</h3>
<p>
 Internet Explorer 6, 7 and 8&nbsp;are no longer supported by Microsoft. For your security, it is recommended that you <a href="http://www.beautyoftheweb.com" target="_blank" title="External link. Opens in a new window.">upgrade Internet Explorer</a> to a newer version, or install <a href="http://firefox.com" target="_blank" title="External link. Opens in a new window.">Mozilla Firefox</a> or <a href="http://www.google.com/chrome" target="_blank" title="External link. Opens in a new window.">Google Chrome</a>.</p>
<p>
 &nbsp;</p>
