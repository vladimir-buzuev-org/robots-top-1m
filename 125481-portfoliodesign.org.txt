                            <span class="mv-item-title"><a href="http://www.portfoliodesign.org/contents/detail/133-graphic-design-portfolio-creation-tips.html" >Graphic Design Portfolio Creation Tips</a></span>                                                

                        <p><u>Resource:</u> adigitaldreamer.com </p><p><em><strong>You've worked
 on some graphic design projects either for paid work, school 
assignments, or on your own. Now you need to showcase your talents so 
you can land a job or sell yourself to clients. You need a portfolio...</strong></em></p><h4>Why Do I Need a Graphic Design Portfolio? </h4><p>A portfolio is a 
showcase of the talent and creativity that you have to offer to who ever
 is looking at it. It needs to show that you are creative, but also 
reflect your attention to detail.&nbsp; </p><h4>How Much Work Do I Show? </h4><p>The
 general tendency for a student is to show as much work as possible. 
Just as long as it looks pretty good, right? They think that these 
designs are miles ahead of what they could do before they were in the 
program, so they include it. This is a huge mistake. I've seen many pfs 
watered down and ruined because they chose quantity over quality. Rather
 choose 12 strong designs over 12 strong and 12 so so designs. The 
employer will remember the 12 average designs the most. Those will stick
 in their minds and your chances go down the tubes. You might as well 
walk out. (Please don't though. LOL) </p>

<h4>What Kind of Graphic Design Portfolio should I Create?</h4><p>There
 are 3 main kinds of graphic design portfolios that students generally 
create. One that is printed, one that is online, and one that is on a 
DVD or CD.<br><br>But which one is the right one to create? How do I 
know I'm choosing the right one? The best way to approach this dilemma 
is to create all 3. Yup. 3 of them.<br><br>You see, some employers want 
to touch and feel and interact with printed media, some want to have a 
DVD that they can plop into their computer and view, and some don't want
 to be bothered with a stack of portfolios, so they prefer a website 
where they are one click away.<br><br>We'll be exploring all 3... </p><p><a href="http://www.adigitaldreamer.com/articles/graphic-design-portfolio.htm" target="_blank">Read More... </a></p>                            <div class="mv-clr"></div>
