    <h2>Featured article: Biologic therapies in the management of uveitis in Behçet’s disease</h2>
    
    <div class="featured__content">
        
        <div class="featured__media">
            
                
<div class="CmsItem CmsImage" data-fragment="14217244">
    <a class="CmsImage_link" href="https://ojrd.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13023-017-0681-6">
        <img class="CmsImage_img" src="//media.springernature.com/lw450/springer-cms/rest/v1/content/14217244/data/v1" alt="New Content Item"/>
    </a>
    
</div>


            
        </div>
        
        <div class="featured__text">
            <p>Behçet’s disease (BD) is a systemic vasculitis characterised by a relapsing remitting course, affecting multiple organ systems. In the eye, it is a cause of potentially blinding inflammation in the form of uveitis. Management of uveitis in BD often requires the use of systemic immunosuppression, in order to reduce disease activity and prevent accumulation of irreversible damage.  In this review, we discuss the most recent research investigating the use of biologic therapy in uveitis due to BD, with consideration of health economics and quality of life outcomes.</p>
            
            <p class="featured__link">
                <a href="https://ojrd.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s13023-017-0681-6"> Read more </a>
            </p>

    <h2>Aims and scope</h2>
    <div class="CmsArticle_body">
        <p><em>Orphanet Journal of Rare Diseases</em> is an open access, peer-reviewed journal that encompasses all aspects of rare diseases and orphan drugs. The journal publishes high-quality reviews on specific rare diseases. In addition, the journal may consider articles on clinical trial outcome reports, either positive or negative, and articles on public health issues in the field of rare diseases and orphan drugs. The journal does not accept case reports.</p>

    <h2>About Orphanet</h2>
    <div class="CmsArticle_body">
        <p><a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/ClinicalLabs.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external"><img alt="" class="float--left" title="" src="//resource-cms.springer.com/springer-cms/rest/v1/content/7069368/data/v1"/></a></p><p></p><p>Orphanet is the reference portal for information on rare diseases and orphan drugs. It offers a range of freely accessible services:</p><ul><li>An inventory of rare diseases and a classification of diseases elaborated using existing published expert classifications.</li><li>An <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/Disease_ProEncyclo.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">encyclopedia of rare diseases</a> in six languages.</li><li>An <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/Drugs.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">inventory of orphan drugs</a> at all stages of development, from EMA (European Medicines Agency) orphan designation to European market authorization.</li><li>A directory of specialised services, providing information on <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/Disease_ProEncyclo.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">specialized clinics</a>, <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/ClinicalLabs.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">medical laboratories</a>, <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/ResearchTrials.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">ongoing research projects</a>, <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/ResearchTrials_ClinicalTrials.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">clinical trials</a>, <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/ResearchTrials_RegistriesMaterials.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">registries</a>, networks, technological platforms and <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/SupportGroup.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">patient organizations</a>, in the field of rare diseases, in each of the countries in Orphanet's network.</li><li>An <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/Disease_DiagnosisAssistance.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">assistance-to-diagnosis tool </a>allowing users to search by signs and symptoms.</li><li>An encyclopedia of recommendations and <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/Disease_Emergency.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">guidelines for emergency medical care</a>.</li><li>A bimonthly newsletter, <a href="http://www.orpha.net/actor/cgi-bin/OAhome.php?Ltr=EuropaNews" target="_self" class="is-external">OrphaNews</a>, which gives an overview of scientific and political current affairs in the field of rare diseases and orphan drugs, in English and French.</li><li>A collection of thematic reports, the <a href="http://www.orpha.net/consor/cgi-bin/Education_Home.php?lng=EN" target="_self" class="is-external">Orphanet Reports Series</a>, focusing on overarching themes, directly downloadable from the website.</li><li>Free access to Orphanet data for research purpose is available at <a href="http://www.orphadata.org/cgi-bin/index.php/" target="_self" class="is-external">www.orphadata.org</a>.</li></ul>

    <h2>About the EC Expert Group on Rare Diseases</h2>
    <div class="CmsArticle_body">
        <p></p><p><img alt="" class="float--left" title="" src="//resource-cms.springer.com/springer-cms/rest/v1/content/6652432/data/v1"/></p><p>The European Commission has supported the development and implementation of European public health policy since 2004 through three successive multi-stakeholder forums: the Rare Disease Task Force (2004-2009), the European Union Committee of Experts on Rare Diseases (EUCERD, 2009-2013), and the Commission Expert Group on Rare Diseases (2014).</p><p></p><p>During its mandate, the <a href="http://www.eucerd.eu/" target="_self" class="is-external">EUCERD </a>published a number of recommendations and reports, as well as an annual report on the <a href="http://www.eucerd.eu/?page_id=163#StateArt" target="_self" class="is-external">State of the Art of Rare Disease Activities in Europe</a>, providing detailed information on the activities in the field at both European and Member State level. A review of the achievements of the EUCERD was <a href="http://www.ojrd.com/content/9/1/30" target="_self" class="is-external">published in OJRD</a> to mark Rare Disease Day 2014.</p><p>The <a href="http://ec.europa.eu/health/rare_diseases/expert_group/index_en.htm" target="_self" class="is-external">Commission Expert Group on Rare Diseases</a>, established via <a href="http://ec.europa.eu/health/rare_diseases/docs/dec_expert_group_2013_en.pdf" target="_self" class="is-external">Commission Decision of 30 July 2013 (2013/C 219/04)</a>, continues the work of the EUCERD. The Expert Group brings together stakeholders in the field of rare diseases and representatives of the EU Member States to:</p><ul><li>Assist the European Commission with the preparation and implementation of community activities in the field of rare diseases, including drawing up guidelines and recommendations;</li><li>Foster exchanges of experience, policies and practices between specialised bodies at member state level, European authorities in the fields of research and public health, and stakeholder groups.</li></ul><p>The reports and recommendations issued by the Expert Group are <a href="http://ec.europa.eu/health/rare_diseases/publications/index_en.htm" target="_self" class="is-external">available online</a>.</p>
