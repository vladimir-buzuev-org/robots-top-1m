    <div class="featured__content">
        
        <div class="featured__media">
            
                
<div class="CmsItem CmsImage" data-fragment="10534634">
    <a class="CmsImage_link" href="http://trialsjournal.biomedcentral.com/articles/supplements/volume-18-supplement-3">
        <img class="CmsImage_img" src="//media.springernature.com/lw450/springer-cms/rest/v1/content/10534634/data/v2" alt="Standard clinican image"/>

            <p>Ensuring research is relevant and usable means attention must be taken to select appropriate outcomes when designing clinical trials. The COMET Initiative has developed this Handbook, collating current evidence for the development, implementation, evaluation and updating of core outcome sets, and provides researchers with the guidance needed for designing and choosing relevant outcomes for their clinical trials.</p>

        <div class="featured__text">

In 1997, Professor Altman received the Bradford Hill Medal for his contributions to medical statistics and a DSc from the University of London, and in 1998 was made Professor of Statistics in Medicine by the University of Oxford. His varied research interests include the use and abuse of statistics in medical research, studies of prognosis, regression modelling, systematic reviews and meta-analysis, randomised trials, and studies of medical measurement.</p>
